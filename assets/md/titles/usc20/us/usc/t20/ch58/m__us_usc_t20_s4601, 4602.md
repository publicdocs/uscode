## §§ 4601, 4602. Repealed. [Pub. L. 100–297, title I, § 1003(e)][/us/pl/100/297/s1003/e], Apr. 28, 1988, [102 Stat. 293][/us/stat/102/293]

    Section 4601, [Pub. L. 99–570, title IV, § 4102][/us/pl/99/570/s4102], Oct. 27, 1986, [100 Stat. 3207–125][/us/stat/100/3207-125], related to Congressional findings.

    Section 4602, [Pub. L. 99–570, title IV, § 4103][/us/pl/99/570/s4103], Oct. 27, 1986, [100 Stat. 3207–125][/us/stat/100/3207-125], related to purpose of chapter.

 __Effective Date of Repeal__ 

    Repeal effective July 1, 1988, see [section 6303 of Pub. L. 100–297][/us/pl/100/297/s6303], set out as an Effective Date of 1988 Amendment note under [section 1071 of this title][/us/usc/t20/s1071].

 __Short Title__ 

    [Pub. L. 99–570, title IV, § 4101][/us/pl/99/570/s4101], Oct. 27, 1986, [100 Stat. 3207–125][/us/stat/100/3207-125], which provided that subtitle B \[§§ 4101 to 4144\] of title IV of [Pub. L. 99–570][/us/pl/99/570] was to be cited as the “Drug-Free Schools and Communities Act of 1986”, was repealed by [Pub. L. 100–297, title I, § 1003(e)][/us/pl/100/297/s1003/e], Apr. 28, 1988, [102 Stat. 293][/us/stat/102/293].

 __White House Conference for a Drug Free America__ 

    [Pub. L. 99–570, title I][/us/pl/99/570], subtitle S (§ 1931 et seq.), Oct. 27, 1986, [100 Stat. 3207–56][/us/stat/100/3207-56], as amended by [Pub. L. 100–138][/us/pl/100/138], Oct. 23, 1987, [101 Stat. 820][/us/stat/101/820], established the White House Conference for a Drug Free America, provided for its purpose, responsibilities, participants, authorization of appropriations, and required a final report to be submitted not later than July 31, 1988, with the President to report annually to Congress for three years thereafter on the status and implementation of the findings and recommendations of the Conference.

 __Executive Order No. 12595__ 

    Ex. Ord. No. 12595, May 5, 1987, 52 F.R. 17383, established and set forth the functions of the White House Conference for a Drug Free America.
