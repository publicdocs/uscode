## § 7101. Short title

    This part may be cited as the “Safe and Drug-Free Schools and Communities Act”.

([Pub. L. 89–10, title IV, § 4001][/us/pl/89/10/s4001], as added [Pub. L. 107–110, title IV, § 401][/us/pl/107/110/s401], Jan. 8, 2002, [115 Stat. 1734][/us/stat/115/1734].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    A prior section 7101, [Pub. L. 89–10, title IV, § 4001][/us/pl/89/10/s4001], as added [Pub. L. 103–382, title I, § 101][/us/pl/103/382/s101], Oct. 20, 1994, [108 Stat. 3672][/us/stat/108/3672], set forth short title of subchapter as the Safe and Drug-Free Schools and Communities Act of 1994, prior to the general amendment of this subchapter by [Pub. L. 107–110][/us/pl/107/110].

    A prior [section 4001 of Pub. L. 89–10][/us/pl/89/10/s4001] was classified to [section 3041 of this title][/us/usc/t20/s3041], prior to the general amendment of [Pub. L. 89–10][/us/pl/89/10] by [Pub. L. 103–382][/us/pl/103/382].

 __Development of Model Program of Strategies and Tactics__ 

    [Pub. L. 101–647, title XV, § 1501][/us/pl/101/647/s1501], Nov. 29, 1990, [104 Stat. 4836][/us/stat/104/4836], required the Attorney General to develop a model program of strategies and tactics for establishing and maintaining drug-free school zones and to submit a report to Congress, at the conclusion of the program, describing the strategies and tactics that were found to be successful in establishing, enforcing, and maintaining drug-free school zones.
