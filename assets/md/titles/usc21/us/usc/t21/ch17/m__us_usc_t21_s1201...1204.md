## §§ 1201 to 1204. Repealed. [Pub. L. 100–690, title I][/us/pl/100/690/tI], § 1007(a)(3), Nov. 18, 1988, [102 Stat. 4187][/us/stat/102/4187]

    Section 1201, [Pub. L. 98–473, title II][/us/pl/98/473/tII], § 1302, Oct. 12, 1984, [98 Stat. 2168][/us/stat/98/2168], set forth Congressional findings and declaration of purpose relating to illegal flow of narcotics into United States.

    Section 1202, [Pub. L. 98–473, title II][/us/pl/98/473/tII], § 1303, Oct. 12, 1984, [98 Stat. 2168][/us/stat/98/2168], established National Drug Enforcement Policy Board.

    Section 1203, [Pub. L. 98–473, title II][/us/pl/98/473/tII], § 1304, Oct. 12, 1984, [98 Stat. 2169][/us/stat/98/2169], delineated responsibilities and functions of National Drug Enforcement Policy Board.

    Section 1204, [Pub. L. 98–473, title II][/us/pl/98/473/tII], § 1305, Oct. 12, 1984, [98 Stat. 2170][/us/stat/98/2170], related to reports to Congress.

 __Effective Date of Repeal__ 

    [Pub. L. 100–690, title I][/us/pl/100/690/tI], § 1007(a)(3), Nov. 18, 1988, [102 Stat. 4187][/us/stat/102/4187], provided that the repeal of this chapter is effective on 30th day after first Director of National Drug Control Policy is confirmed by the Senate.

 __Short Title__ 

    [Pub. L. 98–473, title II][/us/pl/98/473/tII], § 1301, Oct. 12, 1984, [98 Stat. 2168][/us/stat/98/2168], which provided that chapter XIII (§§ 1301 to 1307) of title II of [Pub. L. 98–473][/us/pl/98/473] was to be cited as the National Narcotics Act of 1984, was repealed by [Pub. L. 100–690, title I][/us/pl/100/690/tI], § 1007(a)(3), Nov. 18, 1988, [102 Stat. 4187][/us/stat/102/4187].

 __Executive Order No. 12590__ 

    Ex. Ord. No. 12590, Mar. 26, 1987, 52 F.R. 10021, as amended by Ex. Ord. No. 13284, § 11, Jan. 23, 2003, 68 F.R. 4076, provided for the establishment of a National Drug Policy Board, designated its membership and functions, and authorized coordinating groups.
