## § 321a. “Butter” defined

    For the purposes of the Food and Drug Act of June 30, 1906 (Thirty-fourth Statutes at Large, page 768) “butter” shall be understood to mean the food product usually known as butter, and which is made exclusively from milk or cream, or both, with or without common salt, and with or without additional coloring matter, and containing not less than 80 per centum by weight of milk fat, all tolerances having been allowed for.

([Mar. 4, 1923, ch. 268][/us/act/1923-03-04/ch268], [42 Stat. 1500][/us/stat/42/1500].)

 __References in Text__ 

    The Food and Drug Act of June 30, 1906, referred to in text, is [act June 30, 1906, ch. 3915][/us/act/1906-06-30/ch3915], [34 Stat. 768][/us/stat/34/768], which was classified to subchapter I (§ 1 et seq.) of chapter 1 of this title, was repealed (except for section 14a which was transferred to [section 376 of this title][/us/usc/t21/s376]) by [act June 25, 1938, ch. 675, § 1002(a)][/us/act/1938-06-25/ch675/s1002/a], formerly § 902(a), [52 Stat. 1059][/us/stat/52/1059]; renumbered § 1002(a), [Pub. L. 111–31, div. A, title I, § 101(b)(2)][/us/pl/111/31/s101/b/2], June 22, 2009, [123 Stat. 1784][/us/stat/123/1784], and is covered by this chapter.

 __Codification__ 

    Section, which was not enacted as part of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act which comprises this chapter, was formerly classified to [section 6 of this title][/us/usc/t21/s6]. Section 1002(a) of act June 25, 1938, set out as an Effective Date note under [section 301 of this title][/us/usc/t21/s301], provided that this section should remain in force and effect and be applicable to the provisions of this chapter.
