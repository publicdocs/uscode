## § 157. Inspection

    Any officer, agent, or employee of the Department of Agriculture duly authorized by the Secretary of Agriculture for the purpose may, at any hour during the daytime or nighttime, enter and inspect any establishment where any virus, serum, toxin, or analogous product for use in the treatment of domestic animals is prepared for sale, barter, exchange, or shipment as aforesaid.

([Mar. 4, 1913, ch. 145][/us/act/1913-03-04/ch145], § 1 (part), [37 Stat. 833][/us/stat/37/833]; [Pub. L. 99–198, title XVII][/us/pl/99/198/tXVII], § 1768(d), Dec. 23, 1985, [99 Stat. 1655][/us/stat/99/1655].)

 __Codification__ 

    See note set out under [section 151 of this title][/us/usc/t21/s151].

    Another [section 1768 of Pub. L. 99–198][/us/pl/99/198/s1768], cited as a credit to this section, amended [section 136y of Title 7][/us/usc/t7/s136y], Agriculture.

 __Amendments__ 

    1985—[Pub. L. 99–198][/us/pl/99/198] struck out “licensed under this chapter” after “enter and inspect any establishment”.

 __Effective Date of 1985 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 99–198][/us/pl/99/198] effective Dec. 23, 1985, except as otherwise provided, see [section 1768(f) of Pub. L. 99–198][/us/pl/99/198/s1768/f], set out as a note under [section 151 of this title][/us/usc/t21/s151].

 __Transfer of Functions__ 

    For transfer of functions of the Secretary of Agriculture relating to agricultural import and entry inspection activities under this chapter to the Secretary of Homeland Security, and for treatment of related references, see sections 231, 551(d), 552(d), and 557 of Title 6, Domestic Security, and the Department of Homeland Security Reorganization Plan of November 25, 2002, as modified, set out as a note under [section 542 of Title 6][/us/usc/t6/s542].
