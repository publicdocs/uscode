## § 938. Full faith and credit of the United States

    Any contract of insurance or guarantee executed by the Secretary under this subchapter shall be an obligation supported by the full faith and credit of the United States and incontestable except for fraud or misrepresentation of which the holder had actual knowledge at the time it became a holder.

([May 20, 1936, ch. 432][/us/act/1936-05-20/ch432], title III, § 308, as added [Pub. L. 93–32, § 2][/us/pl/93/32/s2], May 11, 1973, [87 Stat. 70][/us/stat/87/70]; amended [Pub. L. 94–124, § 2][/us/pl/94/124/s2], Nov. 4, 1975, [89 Stat. 677][/us/stat/89/677]; [Pub. L. 103–354, title II, § 235(a)(13)][/us/pl/103/354/s235/a/13], Oct. 13, 1994, [108 Stat. 3221][/us/stat/108/3221].)

 __Amendments__ 

    1994—[Pub. L. 103–354][/us/pl/103/354] substituted “Secretary” for “Administrator”.

    1975—[Pub. L. 94–124][/us/pl/94/124] substituted “of which the holder had actual knowledge at the time it became a holder” for “of which the holder has actual knowledge”.

 __Effective Date__ 

    Section effective May 11, 1973, see [section 12 of Pub. L. 93–32][/us/pl/93/32/s12], set out as a note under [section 930 of this title][/us/usc/t7/s930].
