## § 2230. Employees in Alaska; subsistence, equipment, and supplies

    The Secretary of Agriculture is authorized to furnish subsistence to employees of the United States Department of Agriculture in the Territory of Alaska, and to purchase personal equipment and supplies for them, and to make deductions to meet the cost thereof from any money appropriated for salary payments or otherwise due such employees.

([Feb. 16, 1931, ch. 200][/us/act/1931-02-16/ch200], [46 Stat. 1162][/us/stat/46/1162].)

 __Codification__ 

    Section was formerly classified to [section 543a of Title 5][/us/usc/t5/s543a] prior to the general revision and enactment of Title 5, Government Organization and Employees, by [Pub. L. 89–554, § 1][/us/pl/89/554/s1], Sept. 6, 1966, [80 Stat. 378][/us/stat/80/378].

 __Repeal__ 

    Insofar as the provisions of this section relating to subsistence may conflict with those of sections 821 to 823, and 827 to 833 of former Title 5, which are now covered by sections 102, 105, 2105, 2106, 5701, 5705, 5707 and 5708 of Title 5, Government Organization and Employees, they were repealed by section 829 of former Title 5, which is now covered by [section 5708 of Title 5][/us/usc/t5/s5708].

 __Admission of Alaska as State__ 

    Admission of Alaska into the Union was accomplished Jan. 3, 1959, on issuance of Proc. No. 3269, Jan. 3, 1959, 24 F.R. 81, 73 Stat. c16, as required by sections 1 and 8(c) of [Pub. L. 85–508][/us/pl/85/508], July 7, 1958, 72 Stat 339, set out as notes preceding [section 21 of Title 48][/us/usc/t48/s21], Territories and Insular Possessions.
