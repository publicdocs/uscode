## § 2461. Appeals

    From the decisions made under sections 2404, 2443, 2501, and 2568 of this title appeal may, within sixty days or such further times as the Secretary allows, be taken under the Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure. The United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit shall have jurisdiction of any such appeal.

([Pub. L. 91–577, title II, § 71][/us/pl/91/577/s71], Dec. 24, 1970, [84 Stat. 1550][/us/stat/84/1550]; [Pub. L. 97–164, title I, § 145][/us/pl/97/164/s145], Apr. 2, 1982, [96 Stat. 45][/us/stat/96/45]; [Pub. L. 103–349, § 8(d)(1)][/us/pl/103/349/s8/d/1], Oct. 6, 1994, [108 Stat. 3141][/us/stat/108/3141].)

 __Amendments__ 

    1994—[Pub. L. 103–349][/us/pl/103/349] struck out “2502,” after “2501,”.

    1982—[Pub. L. 97–164][/us/pl/97/164] substituted “The United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit shall have jurisdiction of any such appeal” for “The Court of Customs and Patent Appeals and United States Courts of Appeals shall have jurisdiction, with venue in the case of the latter as stated in [section 2343 of title 28][/us/usc/t28/s2343]”.

 __Effective Date of 1994 Amendment__
