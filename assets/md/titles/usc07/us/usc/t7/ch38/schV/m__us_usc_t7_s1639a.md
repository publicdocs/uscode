## § 1639a. Applicability

    (a) __In general__ 

        This subchapter shall apply to any claim in a disclosure that a food bears that indicates that the food is a bioengineered food.

    (b) __Application of definition__ 

        The definition of the term “bioengineering” under [section 1639 of this title][/us/usc/t7/s1639] shall not affect any other definition, program, rule, or regulation of the Federal Government.

    (c) __Application to foods__ 

    This subchapter shall apply only to a food subject to—

        (1) the labeling requirements under the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act ([21 U.S.C. 301][/us/usc/t21/s301] et seq.); or

        (2)

         the labeling requirements under the Federal Meat Inspection Act ([21 U.S.C. 601][/us/usc/t21/s601] et seq.), the Poultry Products Inspection Act ([21 U.S.C. 451][/us/usc/t21/s451] et seq.), or the Egg Products Inspection Act ([21 U.S.C. 1031][/us/usc/t21/s1031] et seq.) only if—

            (A) the most predominant ingredient of the food would independently be subject to the labeling requirements under the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act ([21 U.S.C. 301][/us/usc/t21/s301] et seq.); or

            (B)

                (i) the most predominant ingredient of the food is broth, stock, water, or a similar solution; and

                (ii) the second-most predominant ingredient of the food would independently be subject to the labeling requirements under the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act ([21 U.S.C. 301][/us/usc/t21/s301] et seq.).

([Aug. 14, 1946, ch. 966][/us/act/1946-08-14/ch966], title II, § 292, as added [Pub. L. 114–216][/us/pl/114/216], § 1, July 29, 2016, [130 Stat. 834][/us/stat/130/834].)
