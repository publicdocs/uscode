## § 3030g. Criteria

    The Assistant Secretary, in consultation with recognized experts in the fields of nutrition science, dietetics, meal planning and food service management, and aging, shall develop minimum criteria of efficiency and quality for the furnishing of home delivered meal services for projects described in [section 3030f of this title][/us/usc/t42/s3030f].

([Pub. L. 89–73, title III][/us/pl/89/73/tIII], § 337, as added [Pub. L. 95–478, title I][/us/pl/95/478/tI], § 103(b), Oct. 18, 1978, [92 Stat. 1536][/us/stat/92/1536]; amended [Pub.  __Amendments__ 

    2006—[Pub. L. 109–365][/us/pl/109/365] amended section generally. Prior to amendment, text read as follows: “The Assistant Secretary, in consultation with organizations of and for the aged, blind, and disabled, and with representatives from the American Dietetic Association, the Dietary Managers Association, the National Association of Area Agencies on Aging, the National Association of Nutrition and Aging Services Programs, the National Association of Meals Programs, Incorporated, and any other appropriate group, shall develop minimum criteria of efficiency and quality for the furnishing of home delivered meal services for projects described in [section 3030f of this title][/us/usc/t42/s3030f]. The criteria required by this section shall take into account the ability of established home delivered meals programs to continue such services without major alteration in the furnishing of such services.”

    1993—[Pub. L. 103–171][/us/pl/103/171] substituted “Assistant Secretary” for “Commissioner”.

    1992—[Pub. L. 102–375][/us/pl/102/375] inserted “the Dietary Managers Association,” after “Dietetic Association,”.

    1987—[Pub. L. 100–175][/us/pl/100/175] substituted “National Association of Area Agencies” for “Association of Area Agencies”.

    1981—[Pub. L. 97–115][/us/pl/97/115] substituted “National Association of Nutrition and Aging Services Programs” for “National Association of Title VII Project Directors”.
