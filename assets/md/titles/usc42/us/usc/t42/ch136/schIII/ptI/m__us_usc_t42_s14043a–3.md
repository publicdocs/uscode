## § 14043a–3. Authorization of appropriations

    (a) __In general__ 

        There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this part $5,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2007 to 2011.

    (b) __Availability__ 

        Funds appropriated under this section shall remain available until expended and may only be used for the specific programs and activities described in this part.

    (c) __Set aside__ 

        (1) __In general__ 

            Not less than 10 percent of the total amount available under this section for each fiscal year shall be available for grants under the program authorized by [section 3796gg–10 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s3796gg–10].

        (2) __Applicability of part__  __ <sup>\[__  __1__  __\]</sup> __  __ <sup><sup> __  __1__  __ So in original. Probably should be “section”.__  __ </sup></sup> __ 

            The requirements of this section shall not apply to funds allocated for the program described in paragraph (1).

([Pub. L. 103–322, title IV, § 41006][/us/pl/103/322/s41006], as added [Pub. L. 109–162, title I, § 105(a)][/us/pl/109/162/s105/a], Jan. 5, 2006, [119 Stat. 2981][/us/stat/119/2981]; amended [Pub. L. 109–271, § 7(c)(2)][/us/pl/109/271/s7/c/2], Aug. 12, 2006, [120 Stat. 765][/us/stat/120/765].)

 __Repeal of Section__ 

For effective date of repeal by [Pub. L. 113–4][/us/pl/113/4], see Effective Date of Repeal note below.

 __Amendments__ 

    2006—Subsec. (c). [Pub. L. 109–271][/us/pl/109/271] added subsec. (c) and struck out former subsec. (c). Prior to amendment, text read as follows: “Of the amounts made available under this subsection in each fiscal year, not less than 10 percent shall be used for grants for tribal courts, tribal court-related programs, and tribal nonprofits.”

 __Effective Date of Repeal__ 

    Repeal not effective until the beginning of the fiscal year following Mar. 7, 2013, see [section 4 of Pub. L. 113–4][/us/pl/113/4/s4], set out as an Effective Date of 2013 Amendment note under [section 2261 of Title 18][/us/usc/t18/s2261], Crimes and Criminal Procedure.
