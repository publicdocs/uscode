## § 193. Assistant chief

    There shall be in the Children’s Bureau, until otherwise provided for by law, an assistant chief, to be appointed by the Secretary of Health and Human Services.

([Apr. 9, 1912, ch. 73, § 3][/us/act/1912-04-09/ch73/s3], [37 Stat. 80][/us/stat/37/80]; [Mar. 4, 1913, ch. 141][/us/act/1913-03-04/ch141], §§ 3, 6, [37 Stat. 737][/us/stat/37/737], 738; 1946 Reorg. Plan No. 2, § 1, eff. July 16, 1946, 11 F.R. 7873, [60 Stat. 1095][/us/stat/60/1095]; 1953 Reorg. Plan No. 1, §§ 5, 8, eff. Apr. 11, 1953, 18 F.R. 2053, [67 Stat. 631][/us/stat/67/631]; [Pub. L. 96–88, title V, § 509(b)][/us/pl/96/88/s509/b], Oct. 17, 1979, [93 Stat. 695][/us/stat/93/695].)

 __Codification__ 

    Section 3 of act Apr. 9, 1912, also provided for compensation of assistant chief and for appointment and compensation of other employees of the bureau.

    Section was formerly classified to [section 18b of Title 29][/us/usc/t29/s18b], Labor.

 __Transfer of Functions__ 

    Functions of Federal Security Administrator transferred to Secretary of Health, Education, and Welfare and all agencies of Federal Security Agency transferred to Department of Health, Education, and Welfare by section 5 of Reorg. Plan No. 1 of 1953, set out in as a note under [section 3501 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s3501]. Federal Security Agency and office of Administrator abolished by section 8 of Reorg. Plan No. 1 of 1953. Secretary and Department of Health, Education, and Welfare redesignated Secretary and Department of Health and Human Services by [section 509(b) of Pub. L. 96–88][/us/pl/96/88/s509/b] which is classified to [section 3508(b) of Title 20][/us/usc/t20/s3508/b], Education.

    “Federal Security Administrator” substituted for “Secretary of Labor” pursuant to Reorg. Plan No. 2 of 1946. See note set out under [section 191 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s191].

    “Secretary of Labor” substituted for “Secretary of Commerce and Labor” pursuant to act Mar. 4, 1913. See note set out under [section 191 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s191].
