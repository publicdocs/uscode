## § 12202. State immunity

    A State shall not be immune under the eleventh amendment to the Constitution of the United States from an action in  <sup>\[1\]</sup>  <sup><sup> 1 So in original. Probably should be “in a”. </sup></sup>  Federal or State court of competent jurisdiction for a violation of this chapter. In any action against a State for a violation of the requirements of this chapter, remedies (including remedies both at law and in equity) are available for such a violation to the same extent as such remedies are available for such a violation in an action against any public or private entity other than a State.

([Pub. L. 101–336, title V][/us/pl/101/336/tV], § 502, July 26, 1990, [104 Stat. 370][/us/stat/104/370].)

 __References in Text__ 

    This chapter, referred to in text, was in the original “this Act”, meaning [Pub. L. 101–336][/us/pl/101/336], July 26, 1990, [104 Stat. 327][/us/stat/104/327], which is classified principally to this chapter. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see Short Title note set out under [section 12101 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s12101] and Tables.
