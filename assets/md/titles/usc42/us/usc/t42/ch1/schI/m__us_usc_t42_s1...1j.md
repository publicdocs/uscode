## §§ 1 to 1j. Repealed. [July 1, 1944, ch. 373][/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373], title XIII, § 1313, [58 Stat. 714][/us/stat/58/714]

    Section 1, acts [July 1, 1902, ch. 1370][/us/act/1902-07-01/ch1370], § 1, [32 Stat. 712][/us/stat/32/712]; [Aug. 14, 1912, ch. 288][/us/act/1912-08-14/ch288], § 1, [37 Stat. 309][/us/stat/37/309], provided that Public Health and Marine Hospital Service should be known as the Public Health Service. See [section 202 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s202].

    Section 1a, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 1, [57 Stat. 587][/us/stat/57/587], provided for organization and function of Public Health Service. See [section 203 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s203].

    Section 1b, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 2, [57 Stat. 587][/us/stat/57/587], provided for appointment of Assistant Surgeons General, their grade, pay, and allowances. See sections 206, 207, and 210 of this title.

    Section 1c, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 3, [57 Stat. 587][/us/stat/57/587], provided for chiefs of divisions, their grade, pay and allowances, and creation of a Dental Division and a Sanitary Engineering Division. See sections 206, 207, and 210 of this title.

    Section 1d, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 4, [57 Stat. 587][/us/stat/57/587], provided for temporary promotions in regular corps in time of war. See [section 211 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s211].

    Section 1e, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 5, [58 Stat. 588][/us/stat/58/588], provided for review of record of officers above grade of assistant surgeon and their separation from service. See [section 211 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s211].

    Section 1f, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 6, [58 Stat. 588][/us/stat/58/588], provided for an acting Surgeon General during absence of Surgeon General and Assistant to Surgeon General. See [section 206 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s206].

    Section 1g, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 7, [57 Stat. 588][/us/stat/57/588], provided for death and disability benefits of commissioned officers during war and for transfer of Service to military forces. See sections 213 and 217 of this title.

    Section 1h, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 8, [57 Stat. 589][/us/stat/57/589], provided for commissioned officers’ benefits as civil officers and employees of United States and election of benefits. See Title 5, Government Organization and Employees.

    Section 1i, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 9, [57 Stat. 589][/us/stat/57/589], provided for beneficiaries’ benefits where commissioned officer lost his life on active duty between Dec. 7, 1941, and Nov. 11, 1943.

    Section 1j, [act Nov. 11, 1943, ch. 298][/us/act/1943-11-11/ch298], § 11, [57 Stat. 589][/us/stat/57/589], provided for transfer of appropriations to continue transferred functions. See note set out under [section 201 of this title][/us/usc/t42/s201].
