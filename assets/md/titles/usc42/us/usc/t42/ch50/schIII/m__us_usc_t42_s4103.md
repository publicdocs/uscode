## § 4103. Repealed. [Pub. L. 103–325, title V][/us/pl/103/325/tV], § 551(a), Sept. 23, 1994, [108 Stat. 2269][/us/stat/108/2269]

    Section, [Pub. L. 90–448, title XIII][/us/pl/90/448/tXIII], § 1362, Aug. 1, 1968, [82 Stat. 588][/us/stat/82/588]; [Pub. L. 95–128, title VII][/us/pl/95/128/tVII], § 704(b), Oct. 12, 1977, [91 Stat. 1145][/us/stat/91/1145]; [Pub. L. 98–181, title I][/us/pl/98/181/tI] \[title IV, § 451(d)(1)\], Nov. 30, 1983, [97 Stat. 1229][/us/stat/97/1229], related to purchase, by Director, of insured properties damaged substantially beyond repair by flood, for subsequent transfer to State or local agency upon negotiated conditions.

 __Savings Provision__ 

    [Pub. L. 103–325, title V][/us/pl/103/325/tV], § 551(c), Sept. 23, 1994, [108 Stat. 2269][/us/stat/108/2269], required the Director of the Federal Emergency Management Agency to comply with any purchase or loan commitment entered into before the expiration of the 1-year period beginning on Sept. 23, 1994, pursuant to authority under this section or [section 551(b) of Pub. L. 103–325][/us/pl/103/325/s551/b].

 __Transition Phase__ 

    [Pub. L. 103–325, title V][/us/pl/103/325/tV], § 551(b), Sept. 23, 1994, [108 Stat. 2269][/us/stat/108/2269], permitted the Director of the Federal Emergency Management Agency to enter into loan and purchase commitments as provided under this section (as in effect immediately before the enactment of [Pub. L. 103–325][/us/pl/103/325]) during the 1-year period beginning on Sept. 23, 1994.
