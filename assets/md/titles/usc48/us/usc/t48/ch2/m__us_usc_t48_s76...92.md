## §§ 76 to 92. Omitted

 __Codification__ 

    Sections 76 to 92, relating to Territory of Alaska, were omitted in view of admission of Alaska into the Union.

    Section 76, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 8, [37 Stat. 514][/us/stat/37/514], specified enacting clause of all laws, and provided that no law shall embrace more than one subject.

    Section 77, acts [Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 9, [37 Stat. 514][/us/stat/37/514]; [Apr. 13, 1934, ch. 119][/us/act/1934-04-13/ch119], § 2, [48 Stat. 583][/us/stat/48/583]; [June 3, 1948, ch. 396][/us/act/1948-06-03/ch396], [62 Stat. 302][/us/stat/62/302], detailed general legislative power and limitation.

    Section 78, acts [Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 9, [37 Stat. 514][/us/stat/37/514]; [June 3, 1948, ch. 396][/us/act/1948-06-03/ch396], [62 Stat. 302][/us/stat/62/302], required all taxes to be uniform.

    Section 79, acts [Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 9, [37 Stat. 514][/us/stat/37/514]; [June 3, 1948, ch. 396][/us/act/1948-06-03/ch396], [62 Stat. 302][/us/stat/62/302], limited amount of taxes for Territorial purposes.

    Section 80, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 3, [37 Stat. 512][/us/stat/37/512], prohibited legislature from passing laws depriving judges, officers, etc. of district court of authority or jurisdiction.

    Section 81, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 10, [37 Stat. 515][/us/stat/37/515], related to rules of legislature, quorum and majority.

    Section 82, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 11, [37 Stat. 516][/us/stat/37/516], prohibited members of legislature from holding other office.

    Section 83, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 11, [37 Stat. 516][/us/stat/37/516], prohibited persons holding appointment under the United States from being members of legislature or holding other Territorial office.

    Section 84, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 12, [37 Stat. 516][/us/stat/37/516], specified exemptions and privileges of members of legislature.

    Section 85, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 13, [37 Stat. 516][/us/stat/37/516], described manner of passage of laws.

    Section 86, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 14, [37 Stat. 516][/us/stat/37/516], related to veto power of governor.

    Section 87, acts [Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 15, [37 Stat. 516][/us/stat/37/516]; [Nov. 13, 1942, ch. 637][/us/act/1942-11-13/ch637], § 3, [56 Stat. 1018][/us/stat/56/1018], provided for legislative expenses.

    Section 88, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 16, [37 Stat. 517][/us/stat/37/517], directed transmission of copies of law to the President and Secretary of State.

    Section 89, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 16, [37 Stat. 517][/us/stat/37/517], required legislature to make provision for printing of laws and distribution in Territory.

    Section 90, [act Aug. 24, 1912, ch. 387][/us/act/1912-08-24/ch387], § 20, [37 Stat. 518][/us/stat/37/518], required all Territorial laws to be submitted to Congress.

    Section 91, [act Aug. 29, 1914, ch. 292][/us/act/1914-08-29/ch292], [38 Stat. 710][/us/stat/38/710], related to powers of courts and legislature.

    Section 92, [act Feb. 18, 1929, ch. 260][/us/act/1929-02-18/ch260], [45 Stat. 1228][/us/stat/45/1228], directed all appropriations by legislature to be in conformity with sections 23, 24, 67 to 73, and 74 to 90 of this title.
