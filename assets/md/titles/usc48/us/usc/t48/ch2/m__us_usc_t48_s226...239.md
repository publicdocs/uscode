## §§ 226 to 239. Omitted

 __Codification__ 

    Sections 226 to 239, relating to Territory of Alaska, were omitted in view of admission of Alaska into the Union.

    Section 226, [act June 6, 1924, ch. 272][/us/act/1924-06-06/ch272], § 6, [43 Stat. 466][/us/stat/43/466], prescribed penalties for violation of fishing laws.

    Section 227, [act June 6, 1924, ch. 272][/us/act/1924-06-06/ch272], § 6, [43 Stat. 466][/us/stat/43/466], empowered Director of Fish and Wildlife Service to designate employees as peace officers.

    Section 228, [act June 6, 1924, ch. 272][/us/act/1924-06-06/ch272], § 8, [43 Stat. 467][/us/stat/43/467], provided that nothing in sections 221 to 224, 226 to 228, and 232 to 234 of this title shall not abrogate or curtail any territorial powers.

    Section 229, [act May 31, 1920, ch. 217][/us/act/1920-05-31/ch217], [41 Stat. 717][/us/stat/41/717], enumerated powers of bird reservation wardens.

    Section 230, [act June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 1, [34 Stat. 478][/us/stat/34/478], established a license tax on canning fish.

    Section 231, acts [June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 2, [34 Stat. 478][/us/stat/34/478]; [Mar. 4, 1913, ch. 141][/us/act/1913-03-04/ch141], § 1, [37 Stat. 736][/us/stat/37/736], listed exemptions from license tax.

    Section 232, acts [June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 4, [34 Stat. 479][/us/stat/34/479]; [June 6, 1924, ch. 272][/us/act/1924-06-06/ch272], § 4, [43 Stat. 466][/us/stat/43/466], prescribed manner of taking fish.

    Section 233, acts [June 26, 1900, ch. 3547][/us/act/1900-06-26/ch3547], § 3, [34 Stat. 479][/us/stat/34/479]; [June 6, 1924, ch. 272][/us/act/1924-06-06/ch272], § 3, [43 Stat. 465][/us/stat/43/465]; [Apr. 16, 1934, ch. 146][/us/act/1934-04-16/ch146], § 1, [48 Stat. 594][/us/stat/48/594]; [Mar. 16, 1955, ch. 12][/us/act/1955-03-16/ch12], [69 Stat. 12][/us/stat/69/12], prohibited obstructions in waters for capturing salmon.

    Section 234, acts [June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 5, [34 Stat. 479][/us/stat/34/479]; [June 6, 1924, ch. 272][/us/act/1924-06-06/ch272], § 5, [43 Stat. 466][/us/stat/43/466]; [July 2, 1940, ch. 514][/us/act/1940-07-02/ch514], [54 Stat. 723][/us/stat/54/723], related to closed season for salmon and to stationary and floating traps.

    Section 235, acts [June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 7, [34 Stat. 480][/us/stat/34/480]; [Feb. 28, 1929, ch. 365][/us/act/1929-02-28/ch365], [45 Stat. 1349][/us/stat/45/1349], required salmon to be canned or salted within forty-eight hours after being killed.

    Section 236, [act June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 8, [34 Stat. 480][/us/stat/34/480], prohibited waste or destruction of food fish.

    Section 237, [act June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 9, [34 Stat. 480][/us/stat/34/480], prohibited false labeling or branding of packages of fish.

    Section 238, acts [June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 10, [34 Stat. 480][/us/stat/34/480]; [Mar. 4, 1913, ch. 141][/us/act/1913-03-04/ch141], § 1, 37, Stat. 736, required reports of persons engaged in fishing industry.

    Section 239, acts [June 26, 1906, ch. 3547][/us/act/1906-06-26/ch3547], § 11, [34 Stat. 480][/us/stat/34/480]; [Mar. 4, 1913, ch. 141][/us/act/1913-03-04/ch141], § 1, [37 Stat. 736][/us/stat/37/736], related to manner of catching or killing fish.
