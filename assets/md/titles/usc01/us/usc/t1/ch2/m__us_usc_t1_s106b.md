## § 106b. Amendments to Constitution

    Whenever official notice is received at the National Archives and Records Administration that any amendment proposed to the Constitution of the United States has been adopted, according to the provisions of the Constitution, the Archivist of the United States shall forthwith cause the amendment to be published, with his certificate, specifying the States by which the same may have been adopted, and that the same has become valid, to all intents and purposes, as a part of the Constitution of the United States.

(Added [Oct. 31, 1951, ch. 655, § 2(b)][/us/act/1951-10-31/ch655/s2/b], [65 Stat. 710][/us/stat/65/710]; amended [Pub. L. 98–497, title I, § 107(d)][/us/pl/98/497/s107/d], Oct. 19, 1984, [98 Stat. 2291][/us/stat/98/2291].)

 __Amendments__ 

    1984—[Pub. L. 98–497][/us/pl/98/497] substituted “National Archives and Records Administration” and “Archivist of the United States” for “General Services Administration” and “Administrator of General Services”, respectively.

 __Effective Date of 1984 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 98–497][/us/pl/98/497] effective Apr. 1, 1985, see [section 301 of Pub. L. 98–497][/us/pl/98/497/s301], set out as a note under [section 2102 of Title 44][/us/usc/t44/s2102], Public Printing and Documents.
