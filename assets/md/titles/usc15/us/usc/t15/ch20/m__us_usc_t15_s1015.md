## § 1015. “State” defined

    As used in this chapter, the term “State” includes the several States, Alaska, Hawaii, Puerto Rico, Guam, and the District of Columbia.

([Mar. 9, 1945, ch. 20, § 5][/us/act/1945-03-09/ch20/s5], [59 Stat. 34][/us/stat/59/34]; [Aug. 1, 1956, ch. 852, § 4][/us/act/1956-08-01/ch852/s4], [70 Stat. 908][/us/stat/70/908].)

 __Amendments__ 

    1956—Act Aug. 1, 1956, included “Guam” in definition of State.

 __Admission of Alaska and Hawaii to Statehood__ 

    Alaska was admitted into the Union on Jan. 3, 1959, on issuance of Proc. No. 3269, Jan. 3, 1959, 24 FR 81, 73 Stat. c16, and Hawaii was admitted into the Union on Aug. 21, 1959, on issuance of Proc. No. 3309, Aug. 21, 1959, 24 FR 6868, 73 Stat. c74. For Alaska Statehood Law, see [Pub. L. 85–508][/us/pl/85/508], July 7, 1958, [72 Stat. 339][/us/stat/72/339], set out as a note preceding [section 21 of Title 48][/us/usc/t48/s21], Territories and Insular Possessions. For Hawaii Statehood Law, see [Pub. L. 86–3][/us/pl/86/3], Mar. 18, 1959, [73 Stat. 4][/us/stat/73/4], set out as a note preceding [section 491 of Title 48][/us/usc/t48/s491].
