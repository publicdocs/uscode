## § 1196. Penalties

Violation of section 1192 or 1197(b) of this title, or failure to comply with [section 1202(c) of this title][/us/usc/t15/s1202/c], is punishable by—

    (1) imprisonment for not more than 5 years for a knowing and willful violation of that section;

    (2) a fine determined under [section 3571 of title 18][/us/usc/t18/s3571]; or

    (3) both.

([June 30, 1953, ch. 164, § 7][/us/act/1953-06-30/ch164/s7], [67 Stat. 114][/us/stat/67/114]; [Pub. L. 95–631, § 8(b)][/us/pl/95/631/s8/b], Nov. 10, 1978, [92 Stat. 3747][/us/stat/92/3747]; [Pub. L. 110–314, title II, § 217(c)(4)][/us/pl/110/314/s217/c/4], Aug. 14, 2008, [122 Stat. 3060][/us/stat/122/3060].)

 __Amendments__ 

    2008—[Pub. L. 110–314][/us/pl/110/314] amended section generally. Prior to amendment, text read as follows: “Any person who willfully violates section 1192 or 1197(b) of this title, or who fails to comply with [section 1202(c) of this title][/us/usc/t15/s1202/c], shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not more than $5,000 or be imprisoned not more than one year or both in the discretion of the court: Provided, That nothing herein shall limit other provisions of this chapter.”

    1978—[Pub. L. 95–631][/us/pl/95/631] authorized penalties for noncompliance with [section 1202(c) of this title][/us/usc/t15/s1202/c].
