## § 1070. Appeals to Trademark Trial and Appeal Board from decisions of examiners

    An appeal may be taken to the Trademark Trial and Appeal Board from any final decision of the examiner in charge of the registration of marks upon the payment of the prescribed fee.

([July 5, 1946, ch. 540][/us/act/1946-07-05/ch540], title I, § 20, [60 Stat. 435][/us/stat/60/435]; [Pub. L. 85–609, § 1(b)][/us/pl/85/609/s1/b], Aug. 8, 1958, [72 Stat. 540][/us/stat/72/540].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    [Act Feb. 20, 1905, ch. 592][/us/act/1905-02-20/ch592], §§ 8, 13, [33 Stat. 726][/us/stat/33/726], 728.

 __Amendments__ 

    1958—[Pub. L. 85–609][/us/pl/85/609] substituted “Trademark Trial and Appeal Board” for “Commissioner in person” and “fee” for “fees”, and struck out “of interferences or” after “examiner in charge”.

 __Effective Date of 1958 Amendment__ 

    For effective date and applicability of amendment by [Pub. L. 85–609][/us/pl/85/609], see [section 3 of Pub. L. 85–609][/us/pl/85/609/s3], set out as a note under [section 1067 of this title][/us/usc/t15/s1067].

 __Repeal and Effect on Existing Rights__ 

    Repeal of inconsistent provisions, effect of this chapter on pending proceedings and existing registrations and rights under prior acts, see notes set out under [section 1051 of this title][/us/usc/t15/s1051].
