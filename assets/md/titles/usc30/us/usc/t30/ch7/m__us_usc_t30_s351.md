## § 351. Definitions

    As used in this chapter “United States” includes Alaska. “Acquired lands” or “lands acquired by the United States” include all lands heretofore or hereafter acquired by the United States to which the “mineral leasing laws” have not been extended, including such lands acquired under the provisions of the Act of March 1, 1911 ([36 Stat. 961][/us/stat/36/961], 16 U.S.C., sec. 552). “Secretary” means the Secretary of the Interior, “Mineral leasing laws” shall mean the Act of October 20, 1914 ([38 Stat. 741][/us/stat/38/741], 48 U.S.C., sec. 432); the Act of February 25, 1920 ([41 Stat. 437][/us/stat/41/437], 30 U.S.C., sec. 181); the Act of April 17, 1926 ([44 Stat. 301][/us/stat/44/301], 30 U.S.C., sec. 271); the Act of February 7, 1927 ([44 Stat. 1057][/us/stat/44/1057], 30 U.S.C., sec. 281), and all Acts heretofore or hereafter enacted which are amendatory of or supplementary to any of the foregoing Acts. “Lease” includes “prospecting permit” unless the context otherwise requires. The term “oil” shall embrace all nongaseous hydrocarbon substances other than those leasable as coal, oil shale, or gilsonite (including all vein-type solid hydrocarbons).

([Aug. 7, 1947, ch. 513][/us/act/1947-08-07/ch513], § 2, [61 Stat. 913][/us/stat/61/913]; [Pub. L. 97–78][/us/pl/97/78], § 1(9)(a), Nov. 16, 1981, [95 Stat. 1072][/us/stat/95/1072].)

 __Admission of Alaska as State__ 

    Admission of Alaska into the Union was accomplished Jan. 3, 1959, on issuance of Proc. No. 3269, Jan. 3, 1959, 24 F.R. 81, 73 Stat. c16, as required by sections 1 and 8(c) of [Pub. L. 85–508][/us/pl/85/508], July 7, 1958, [72 Stat. 339][/us/stat/72/339], set out as notes preceding [section 21 of Title 48][/us/usc/t48/s21], Territories and Insular Possessions.

 __Outer Continental Shelf Lands; Definition__ 

    Definition of “outer Continental Shelf” with respect to jurisdiction of United States, and mineral leases on submerged lands of such shelf, see section 1331 et seq. of Title 43, Public Lands.
