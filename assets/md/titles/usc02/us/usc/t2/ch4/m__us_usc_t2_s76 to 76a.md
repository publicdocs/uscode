## §§ 76 to 76a. Repealed. [Pub. L. 104–186, title II, § 204(21)][/us/pl/104/186/s204/21], (22)(A)(iii), (23), Aug. 20, 1996, [110 Stat. 1733][/us/stat/110/1733]

    Section 76, R.S. § 73, related to duties of Doorkeeper of House. Provisions of R.S. § 73 which related to duties of Doorkeeper of Senate were classified to [section 63 of this title][/us/usc/t2/s63] prior to repeal by [Pub. L. 104–186][/us/pl/104/186].

    Section 76–1, based on H. Res. No. 8, par. (3), Ninety-fifth Congress, Jan. 4, 1977, enacted into permanent law by [Pub. L. 95–94, title I, § 115][/us/pl/95/94/s115], Aug. 5, 1977, [91 Stat. 668][/us/stat/91/668], related to compensation of Doorkeeper of House.

    A prior section 76–1 was based on provisions of H. Res. No. 890, Ninety-second Congress, Oct. 4, 1972, enacted into permanent law by [Pub. L. 92–607][/us/pl/92/607], ch. V, Oct. 31, 1972, [86 Stat. 1509][/us/stat/86/1509], relating to compensation of the Doorkeeper being equal to the annual rate of basic pay fixed for level IV of the Executive Schedule under [section 5315 of Title 5][/us/usc/t5/s5315], Government Organization and Employees, prior to those provisions being struck out by [Pub. L. 104–186, title II, § 204(22)(B)][/us/pl/104/186/s204/22/B], Aug. 20, 1996, [110 Stat. 1733][/us/stat/110/1733].

    Another prior section 76–1, based on H. Res. No. 909, Eighty-ninth Congress, Sept. 8, 1966, enacted into permanent law by [Pub. L. 89–697][/us/pl/89/697], ch. VI, § 601, Oct. 27, 1966, [80 Stat. 1064][/us/stat/80/1064], set forth the compensation of the Doorkeeper at equal to the gross per annum rate of compensation of the Clerk of House and Sergeant at Arms of House, prior to being repealed by [Pub. L. 104–186, title II, § 204(22)(B)][/us/pl/104/186/s204/22/B], Aug. 20, 1996, [110 Stat. 1733][/us/stat/110/1733].

    Section 76a, based on H. Res. No. 560, Eighty-seventh Congress, Mar. 27, 1962, enacted into permanent law by [Pub. L. 87–730, § 103][/us/pl/87/730/s103], Oct. 2, 1962, [76 Stat. 693][/us/stat/76/693], related to position of a special assistant in Office of Doorkeeper.
