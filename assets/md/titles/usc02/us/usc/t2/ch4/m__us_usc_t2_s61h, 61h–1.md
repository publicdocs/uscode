## §§ 61h, 61h–1. Omitted

 __Codification__ 

    Section 61h, [Pub. L. 93–371, § 4][/us/pl/93/371/s4], Aug. 13, 1974, [88 Stat. 429][/us/stat/88/429]; [Pub. L. 94–59, title I, § 105][/us/pl/94/59/s105], July 25, 1975, [89 Stat. 275][/us/stat/89/275], set forth maximum annual rate of compensation for Assistant Secretaries for Senate Majority and Minority. [Pub. L. 95–94, title I][/us/pl/95/94], Aug. 5, 1977, [91 Stat. 658][/us/stat/91/658], abolished such positions, effective Oct. 1, 1977, and authorized Secretaries concerned to appoint and fix compensation of such employees as they deem appropriate. See [section 61g–5 of this title][/us/usc/t2/s61g–5].

    A prior section 61h, acts [Aug. 5, 1955, ch. 568][/us/act/1955-08-05/ch568], [69 Stat. 502][/us/stat/69/502]; [June 27, 1956, ch. 453][/us/act/1956-06-27/ch453], [70 Stat. 357][/us/stat/70/357]; Aug. 21, 1959, [Pub. L. 86–176][/us/pl/86/176], [73 Stat. 399][/us/stat/73/399]; Aug. 10, 1961, [Pub. L. 87–130][/us/pl/87/130], [75 Stat. 321][/us/stat/75/321]; July 27, 1965, [Pub. L. 89–90][/us/pl/89/90], [79 Stat. 266][/us/stat/79/266], authorized basic per annum compensation of Assistant Secretaries for Senate Majority and Minority to be fixed by the respective Secretaries.

    Section 61h–1, [Pub. L. 93–371, § 4][/us/pl/93/371/s4], Aug. 13, 1974, [88 Stat. 429][/us/stat/88/429]; [Pub. L. 94–59, title I, § 105][/us/pl/94/59/s105], July 25, 1975, [89 Stat. 275][/us/stat/89/275], set a maximum annual rate of compensation of $38,000 for administrative assistants in Offices of Senate Majority and Minority Leaders. Positions established by Legislative Branch Appropriation Act, 1970, for Offices of Senate Majority and Minority Leaders, which Act, [Pub. L. 91–145][/us/pl/91/145], Dec. 12, 1969, [83 Stat. 339][/us/stat/83/339], formerly classified to this section, authorized respective leaders to appoint an administrative assistant, were abolished, see title I of [Pub. L. 95–26][/us/pl/95/26], [91 Stat. 80][/us/stat/91/80], set out below. See, also, [section 61h–4 of this title][/us/usc/t2/s61h–4].

    A prior section 61h–1, [Pub. L. 91–145][/us/pl/91/145], Dec. 12, 1969, [83 Stat. 339][/us/stat/83/339], authorized Senate Majority and Minority Leaders to each appoint and fix compensation of an administrative assistant, a legislative assistant, an executive secretary, and a clerical assistant in lieu of positions heretofore authorized by Senate Resolution 158, agreed to December 9, 1941, [Pub. L. 86–30][/us/pl/86/30], approved May 20, 1959, and Senate Resolution 240, agreed to January 24, 1952.

 __Abolition of Positions in Offices of Senate Majority and Minority Leaders__ 

[Pub. L. 95–26, title I][/us/pl/95/26], May 4, 1977, [91 Stat. 80][/us/stat/91/80], provided in part: 

> “That the positions established by the Legislative Branch Appropriation Act, 1970 \[[Pub. L. 91–145][/us/pl/91/145], Dec. 12, 1969, [83 Stat. 338][/us/stat/83/338]\], for the Offices of the Majority and Minority Leaders \[of the Senate\] are abolished effective April 1, 1977.”

 The positions referred to were enumerated in [Pub. L. 91–145][/us/pl/91/145], Dec. 12, 1969, [83 Stat. 339][/us/stat/83/339], classified to former [section 61h–1 of this title][/us/usc/t2/s61h–1], which authorized the respective leaders to appoint an administrative assistant, a legislative assistant, an executive secretary, and a clerical assistant in lieu of the positions authorized prior thereto by Senate Resolution 158, agreed to Dec. 9, 1941, [Pub. L. 86–30][/us/pl/86/30], approved May 20, 1959, and Senate Resolution 240, agreed to Jan. 24, 1952. See [section 61h–4 of this title][/us/usc/t2/s61h–4].
