## § 136. Librarian of Congress; appointment; rules and regulations

    The Librarian of Congress shall be appointed by the President, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate. He shall make rules and regulations for the government of the Library.

([Feb. 19, 1897, ch. 265, § 1][/us/act/1897-02-19/ch265/s1], [29 Stat. 544][/us/stat/29/544], 546; [Pub. L. 92–310, title II, § 220(f)][/us/pl/92/310/s220/f], June 6, 1972, [86 Stat. 204][/us/stat/86/204].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    R.S. §§ 88, 89, 4950, which were repealed by acts [Feb. 28, 1933, ch. 131, § 1][/us/act/1933-02-28/ch131/s1], [47 Stat. 1349][/us/stat/47/1349]; [Mar. 3, 1933, ch. 202, § 1][/us/act/1933-03-03/ch202/s1], [47 Stat. 1428][/us/stat/47/1428], 1431.

 __Amendments__ 

    1972—[Pub. L. 92–310][/us/pl/92/310] struck out provisions which required the Librarian of Congress to give a bond in the sum of $20,000.

§§ 136a, 136a–1. __Omitted__
