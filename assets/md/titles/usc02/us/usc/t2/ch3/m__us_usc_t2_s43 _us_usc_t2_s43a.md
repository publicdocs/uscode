###### §§ 43, 43a. Omitted

 __Codification__ 

    Section 43, acts [July 28, 1866, ch. 296][/us/act/1866-07-28/ch296], § 17, [14 Stat. 323][/us/stat/14/323]; Aug. 11, 1993, [Pub. L. 103–69, title III][/us/pl/103/69/tIII], § 310(a), [107 Stat. 712][/us/stat/107/712], provided for Senators to receive mileage for travel to and from regular sessions, and was omitted from the Code in view of the termination of mileage under this section for Senators by [section 1(a) of Pub. L. 104–53][/us/pl/104/53/s1/a], set out below.

    Section 43a, acts [July 8, 1935, ch. 374][/us/act/1935-07-08/ch374], [49 Stat. 459][/us/stat/49/459]; Aug. 11, 1993, [Pub. L. 103–69, title III][/us/pl/103/69/tIII], § 310(b), [107 Stat. 712][/us/stat/107/712], provided for President of Senate to be paid mileage, and was omitted from the Code in view of the termination of mileage under this section for President of Senate by [section 1(b) of Pub. L. 104–53][/us/pl/104/53/s1/b], set out below.

 __Termination of Mileage for Senators and President of Senate__ 

[Pub. L. 104–53, title I][/us/pl/104/53/tI], § 1, Nov. 19, 1995, [109 Stat. 517][/us/stat/109/517], provided that:

>     “(a) On and after October 1, 1995, no Senator shall receive mileage under [section 17 of the Act of July 28, 1866][/us/act/1866-07-28/s17] ([2 U.S.C. 43][/us/usc/t2/s43]).

>     “(b) On and after October 1, 1995, the President of the Senate shall not receive mileage under the first section of the Act of July 8, 1935 ([2 U.S.C. 43a][/us/usc/t2/s43a]).”
