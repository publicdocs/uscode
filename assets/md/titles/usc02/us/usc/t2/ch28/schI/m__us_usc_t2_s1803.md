## § 1803. Delegation of authority

    The Architect of the Capitol may delegate to the assistants of the Architect such authority of the Architect as the Architect may determine proper, except those authorities, duties, and responsibilities specifically assigned to the Deputy Architect of the Capitol by the Legislative Branch Appropriations Act, 2003.

([Aug. 5, 1955, ch. 568][/us/act/1955-08-05/ch568], [69 Stat. 515][/us/stat/69/515]; [Pub. L. 108–7, div. H, title I][/us/pl/108/7/dH/tI], § 1205, Feb. 20, 2003, [117 Stat. 375][/us/stat/117/375].)

 __References in Text__ 

    The Legislative Branch Appropriations Act, 2003, referred to in text, is div. H of [Pub. L. 108–7][/us/pl/108/7], Feb. 20, 2003, [117 Stat. 345][/us/stat/117/345]. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see Tables.

 __Codification__ 

    Section was classified to section 163b of former Title 40, prior to the enactment of Title 40, Public Buildings, Property, and Works, by [Pub. L. 107–217][/us/pl/107/217], § 1, Aug. 21, 2002, [116 Stat. 1062][/us/stat/116/1062].

    Section is from the Legislative Branch Appropriation Act, 1956.

 __Amendments__ 

    2003—[Pub. L. 108–7][/us/pl/108/7] substituted “Architect of the Capitol may delegate to the assistants of the Architect such authority of the Architect as the Architect may determine proper, except those authorities, duties, and responsibilities specifically assigned to the Deputy Architect of the Capitol by the Legislative Branch Appropriations Act, 2003” for “Architect of the Capitol is authorized on and after August 5, 1955, to delegate to the Assistant Architect and other assistants such authority of the Architect as he may deem proper”.
