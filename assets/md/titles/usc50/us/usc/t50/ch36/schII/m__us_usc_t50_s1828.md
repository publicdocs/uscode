## § 1828. Civil liability

    An aggrieved person, other than a foreign power or an agent of a foreign power, as defined in section 1801(a) or (b)(1)(A), respectively, of this title, whose premises, property, information, or material has been subjected to a physical search within the United States or about whom information obtained by such a physical search has been disclosed or used in violation of [section 1827 of this title][/us/usc/t50/s1827] shall have a cause of action against any person who committed such violation and shall be entitled to recover—

    (1) actual damages, but not less than liquidated damages of $1,000 or $100 per day for each day of violation, whichever is greater;

    (2) punitive damages; and

    (3) reasonable attorney’s fees and other investigative and litigation costs reasonably incurred.

([Pub. L. 95–511, title III, § 308][/us/pl/95/511/s308], as added [Pub. L. 103–359, title VIII, § 807(a)(3)][/us/pl/103/359/s807/a/3], Oct. 14, 1994, [108 Stat. 3452][/us/stat/108/3452].)
