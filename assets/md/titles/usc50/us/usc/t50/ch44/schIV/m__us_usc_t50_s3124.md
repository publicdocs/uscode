## § 3124. Extraterritorial jurisdiction

    There is jurisdiction over an offense under [section 3121 of this title][/us/usc/t50/s3121] committed outside the United States if the individual committing the offense is a citizen of the United States or an alien lawfully admitted to the United States for permanent residence (as defined in [section 1101(a)(20) of title 8][/us/usc/t8/s1101/a/20]).

([July 26, 1947, ch. 343][/us/act/1947-07-26/ch343], title VI, § 603, formerly § 604, as added [Pub. L. 97–200][/us/pl/97/200], § 2(a), June 23, 1982, [96 Stat. 123][/us/stat/96/123]; renumbered § 603, [Pub. L. 112–277, title III][/us/pl/112/277/tIII], § 310(a)(4)(B), Jan. 14, 2013, [126 Stat. 2475][/us/stat/126/2475].)

 __Codification__ 

    Section was formerly classified to [section 424 of this title][/us/usc/t50/s424] prior to editorial reclassification and renumbering as this section.

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    A prior section 603 of act July 26, 1947, was classified to [section 423 of this title][/us/usc/t50/s423], prior to repeal by [Pub. L. 112–277, title III][/us/pl/112/277/tIII], § 310(a)(4)(A), Jan. 14, 2013, [126 Stat. 2475][/us/stat/126/2475], and editorial reclassification as [section 3123 of this title][/us/usc/t50/s3123].
