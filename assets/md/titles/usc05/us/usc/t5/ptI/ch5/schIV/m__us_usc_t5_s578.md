## § 578. Authority of the arbitrator

    An arbitrator to whom a dispute is referred under this subchapter may—

    (1) regulate the course of and conduct arbitral hearings;

    (2) administer oaths and affirmations;

    (3) compel the attendance of witnesses and production of evidence at the hearing under the provisions of [section 7 of title 9][/us/usc/t9/s7] only to the extent the agency involved is otherwise authorized by law to do so; and

    (4) make awards.

(Added [Pub. L. 101–552][/us/pl/101/552], § 4(b), Nov. 15, 1990, [104 Stat. 2742][/us/stat/104/2742], § 588; renumbered § 578, [Pub. L. 102–354][/us/pl/102/354], § 3(b)(2), Aug. 26, 1992, [106 Stat. 944][/us/stat/106/944].)
