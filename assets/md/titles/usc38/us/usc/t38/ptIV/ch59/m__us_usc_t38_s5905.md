## § 5905. Penalty for certain acts

    Whoever wrongfully withholds from any claimant or beneficiary any part of a benefit or claim allowed and due to the claimant or beneficiary, shall be fined as provided in title 18, or imprisoned not more than one year, or both.

 __Amendments__ 

    2006—[Pub. L. 109–461][/us/pl/109/461] struck out “(1) directly or indirectly solicits, contracts for, charges, or receives, or attempts to solicit, contract for, charge, or receive, any fee or compensation except as provided in sections 5904 or 1984 of this title, or (2)” after “Whoever”.

    1991—[Pub. L. 102–40][/us/pl/102/40] renumbered [section 3405 of this title][/us/usc/t38/s3405] as this section and substituted “5904” for “3404”.

    [Pub. L. 102–83][/us/pl/102/83] substituted “1984” for “784”.

    1988—[Pub. L. 100–687][/us/pl/100/687] substituted “shall be fined as provided in title 18, or imprisoned not more than one year, or both” for “shall be fined not more than $500 or imprisoned at hard labor for not more than two years, or both”.

    1986—[Pub. L. 99–576][/us/pl/99/576] substituted “to the claimant or beneficiary” for “him”.

 __Effective Date of 1988 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 100–687][/us/pl/100/687] effective Sept. 1, 1989, see [section 401(a) of Pub. L. 100–687][/us/pl/100/687/s401/a], set out as an Effective Date note under [section 7251 of this title][/us/usc/t38/s7251].
