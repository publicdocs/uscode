## § 1985. Decisions by the Secretary

    Except in the event of suit as provided in [section 1984 of this title][/us/usc/t38/s1984], or other appropriate court proceedings, all decisions rendered by the Secretary under the provisions of this chapter shall be final and conclusive on all questions of law or fact, and no other official of the United States shall have jurisdiction to review any such decisions.

([Pub. L. 85–857][/us/pl/85/857], Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1167][/us/stat/72/1167], § 785; renumbered § 1985 and amended [Pub. L. 102–83][/us/pl/102/83], §§ 4(b)(1), (2)(E), 5(a), (c)(1), Aug. 6, 1991, [105 Stat. 404–406][/us/stat/105/404-406].)

 __Amendments__ 

    1991—[Pub. L. 102–83, § 5(a)][/us/pl/102/83/s5/a], renumbered [section 785 of this title][/us/usc/t38/s785] as this section.

    [Pub. L. 102–83, § 5(c)(1)][/us/pl/102/83/s5/c/1], substituted “1984” for “784”.

    [Pub. L. 102–83, § 4(b)(1)][/us/pl/102/83/s4/b/1], (2)(E), substituted “Secretary” for “Administrator” in section catchline and in text.
