#### [ACT JUNE 25, 1942, CH. 447][/us/act/1942-06-25/ch447], [56 STAT. 390][/us/stat/56/390]

§§ 781 to 785. __Omitted__ 

 __Codification__ 

    Sections 781 to 785 of this Appendix were omitted as terminated six months after the termination of the national emergency proclaimed by the President on Dec. 16, 1950, which emergency terminated two years from Sept. 14, 1976, pursuant to [section 1601 of Title 50][/us/usc/t50/s1601], War and National Defense.

    Section 781, [act June 25, 1942, ch. 447, § 1][/us/act/1942-06-25/ch447/s1], [56 Stat. 390][/us/stat/56/390], provided that photographing, sketching, mapping, etc., of military or naval reservations, properties, equipment, etc., is unlawful.

    Section 782, [act June 25, 1942, ch. 447, § 2][/us/act/1942-06-25/ch447/s2], [56 Stat. 391][/us/stat/56/391], provided authority to grant permission to photograph, sketch, map, etc., if national defense interests were not adversely affected.

    Section 783, [act June 25, 1942, ch. 447, § 3][/us/act/1942-06-25/ch447/s3], [56 Stat. 391][/us/stat/56/391], provided penalties for violations of sections 781 to 785 of this Appendix.

    Section 784, [act June 25, 1942, ch. 447, § 4][/us/act/1942-06-25/ch447/s4], [56 Stat. 391][/us/stat/56/391]; Proc. No. 2695, eff. July 4, 1946, 11 F.R. 7817, [60 Stat. 1352][/us/stat/60/1352], provided that sections 781 to 785 of this Appendix apply in all places within territory or jurisdiction of United States.

    Section 785, acts [June 25, 1942, ch. 447, § 5][/us/act/1942-06-25/ch447/s5], [56 Stat. 391][/us/stat/56/391]; [June 4, 1953, ch. 97][/us/act/1953-06-04/ch97], [67 Stat. 41][/us/stat/67/41], provided that sections 781 to 785 of this Appendix be effective only until six months after termination of national emergency proclaimed by President on Dec. 16, 1950.

 __EXEMPTION OF CERTAIN ARTICLES FROM IMPORT DUTIES AND TAXES__ 

 __JOINT RES.__  __JUNE 27, 1942__  __, CH. 455,__  __[__  __56 STAT. 461__  __][/us/stat/56/461]__ 

§§ 791 to 795. __Omitted__ 

 __Codification__ 

    Sections 791 to 795 were omitted as terminated pursuant to section 795 of this Appendix.

    Section 791, Joint Res. [June 27, 1942, ch. 455, § 1][/us/act/1942-06-27/ch455/s1], [56 Stat. 461][/us/stat/56/461], provided for importation of articles for members of armed forces of United Nations, other than those of the United States, free of duties, internal-revenue taxes, and customs charges.

    Section 792, Joint Res. [June 27, 1942, ch. 455, § 2][/us/act/1942-06-27/ch455/s2], [56 Stat. 462][/us/stat/56/462], contained similar exemptions for articles for enemy prisoners of war and enemy civilian internees and detainees.

    Section 793, Joint Res. [June 27, 1942, ch. 455, § 3][/us/act/1942-06-27/ch455/s3], [56 Stat. 462][/us/stat/56/462], contained similar exemptions for articles made by members of United Nations armed forces detained as prisoners of war or made by nationals of United States interned or detained by enemy.

    Section 794, Joint Res. [June 27, 1942, ch. 455, § 4][/us/act/1942-06-27/ch455/s4], [56 Stat. 462][/us/stat/56/462], authorized regulations by Secretary of the Treasury governing exemptions under sections 791 to 795 of this Appendix.

    Section 795, Joint Res. [June 27, 1942, ch. 455, § 5][/us/act/1942-06-27/ch455/s5], [56 Stat. 462][/us/stat/56/462]; [Aug. 8, 1947, ch. 515, § 2][/us/act/1947-08-08/ch515/s2], [61 Stat. 917][/us/stat/61/917], provided that sections 791 to 795 of this Appendix should be effective as to articles entered for consumption or withdrawn from warehouse for consumption on or after June 27, 1942, and before July 1, 1948.
