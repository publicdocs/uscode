#### [ACT OCT. 3, 1944, CH. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], [58 STAT. 785][/us/stat/58/785]

§§ 1651, 1652. __Omitted__ 

 __Codification__ 

    Sections 1651 and 1652 were omitted as terminated on June 30, 1947, pursuant to section 603 of act Oct. 3, 1944, set out as a Termination Date note below, and were subsequently repealed by [Pub. L. 89–554, § 8(a)][/us/pl/89/554/s8/a], Sept. 6, 1966, [80 Stat. 652][/us/stat/80/652].

    Section 1651, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title I, § 101, [58 Stat. 785][/us/stat/58/785], related to establishment of Office of War Mobilization and Reconversion.

    Section 1652, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title I, § 102, [58 Stat. 786][/us/stat/58/786], related to creation of an advisory board.

 __Short Title__ 

    [Act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title VI, § 607, [58 Stat. 792][/us/stat/58/792], provided that sections 1651, 1652, 1656 to 1663, 1666, 1667, 1671, and 1676 to 1678 of this Appendix be cited as the “War Mobilization and Reconversion Act of 1944”.

 __Termination Date__ 

    [Act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title VI, § 603, [58 Stat. 792][/us/stat/58/792], provided that the provisions of sections 1651, 1652, 1656 to 1663, 1671, and 1676 to 1678 of this Appendix terminate on June 30, 1947.

 __Separability Clause__ 

    [Act Oct. 4, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-04/ch480], title VI, § 604, [58 Stat. 792][/us/stat/58/792], provided that if any provision of sections 1651, 1652, 1656 to 1663, 1666, 1667, 1671, and 1676 to 1678 of this Appendix, or the application of such provision to any person or circumstance, is held invalid, the remainder of such sections or the application of such provision to persons or circumstances, other than those as to which it is held invalid, shall not be affected thereby.

 __Appropriation__ 

    [Act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title VI, § 602, [58 Stat. 792][/us/stat/58/792], authorized to be appropriated such sums as might be necessary or appropriate to carry out the purposes and provisions of act Oct. 3, 1944.

§§ 1656 to 1663. __Omitted__ 

 __Codification__ 

    Sections 1656 to 1663 were omitted as terminated on June 30, 1947, pursuant to section 603 of act Oct. 3, 1944, set out as a Termination Date note under former section 1652 of this Appendix.

    Section 1656, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title II, § 201, [58 Stat. 787][/us/stat/58/787], related to a prohibition of indefinite military service.

    Section 1657, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title II, § 202, [58 Stat. 787][/us/stat/58/787], related to termination of prime war contracts.

    Section 1658, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title II, § 203, [58 Stat. 787][/us/stat/58/787], related to integration of termination of war contracts with resumption of civilian production.

    Section 1659, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title II, § 204, [58 Stat. 788][/us/stat/58/788], related to small plant participation in civilian production.

    Section 1660, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title II, § 205, [58 Stat. 788][/us/stat/58/788], related to surveys by Attorney General.

    Section 1661, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title III, § 301, [58 Stat. 788][/us/stat/58/788], related to establishment of a Retraining and Reemployment Administration, and was subsequently repealed by [Pub. L. 89–554, § 8(a)][/us/pl/89/554/s8/a], Sept. 6, 1966, [80 Stat. 652][/us/stat/80/652].

    Section 1662, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title III, § 302, [58 Stat. 789][/us/stat/58/789], related to functions of Retraining and Reemployment Administration.

    Section 1663, [act Oct. 3, 1944, ch. 480][/us/act/1944-10-03/ch480], title III, § 303, [58 Stat. 789][/us/stat/58/789], related to employment of Assistant Administrators, officers and employees, and was subsequently repealed by [Pub. L. 89–554, § 8(a)][/us/pl/89/554/s8/a], Sept. 6, 1966, [80 Stat. 652][/us/stat/80/652].
