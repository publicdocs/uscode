## § 5416. Definitions of package and packaging

    For purposes of this subchapter, the term “package” means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container, and the term “packaging” means the filling of any package.

(Added [Pub. L. 85–859, title II, § 201][/us/pl/85/859/s201], Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1390][/us/stat/72/1390]; amended [Pub. L. 91–673, § 3(e)][/us/pl/91/673/s3/e], Jan. 12, 1971, [84 Stat. 2057][/us/stat/84/2057].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    A prior section 5416, [act Aug. 16, 1954, ch. 736][/us/act/1954-08-16/ch736], [68A Stat. 676][/us/stat/68A/676], consisted of provisions similar to those comprising this section, prior to the general revision of this chapter by [Pub. L. 85–859][/us/pl/85/859].

 __Amendments__ 

    1971—[Pub. L. 91–673][/us/pl/91/673] substituted definitions of package and packaging for definitions of bottle and bottling.

 __Effective Date of 1971 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 91–673][/us/pl/91/673] effective on first day of first calendar month which begins more than 90 days after Jan. 12, 1971, see [section 5 of Pub. L. 91–673][/us/pl/91/673/s5], set out as a note under [section 5056 of this title][/us/usc/t26/s5056].
