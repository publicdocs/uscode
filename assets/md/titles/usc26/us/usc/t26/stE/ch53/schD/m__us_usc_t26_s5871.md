## § 5871. Penalties

    Any person who violates or fails to comply with any provisions of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be fined not more than $10,000, or be imprisoned not more than ten years, or both.

(Added [Pub. L. 90–618, title II][/us/pl/90/618/tII], § 201, Oct. 22, 1968, [82 Stat. 1234][/us/stat/82/1234]; amended [Pub. L. 98–473, title II][/us/pl/98/473/tII], § 227, Oct. 12, 1984, [98 Stat. 2030][/us/stat/98/2030].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    A prior section 5871, [act Aug. 16, 1954, ch. 736][/us/act/1954-08-16/ch736], [68A Stat. 729][/us/stat/68A/729], consisted of provisions similar to those comprising this section, prior to the general revision of this chapter by [Pub. L. 90–618][/us/pl/90/618].

    Provisions similar to those comprising this section were contained in prior section 5861, [act Aug. 16, 1954, ch. 736][/us/act/1954-08-16/ch736], [68A Stat. 729][/us/stat/68A/729], prior to the general revision of this chapter by [Pub. L. 90–618][/us/pl/90/618].

 __Amendments__ 

    1984—[Pub. L. 98–473][/us/pl/98/473] struck out “, and shall become eligible for parole as the Board of Parole shall determine” after “or both”.

 __Effective Date of 1984 Amendment__ 

    [Pub. L. 98–473, title II][/us/pl/98/473/tII], § 235(a)(1)(B)(ii)(IV), Oct. 12, 1984, [98 Stat. 2032][/us/stat/98/2032], provided that the amendment made by [section 227 of Pub. L. 98–473][/us/pl/98/473/s227] is effective Oct. 12, 1984.

 __Effective Date__ 

    Section effective on first day of first month following October 1968, see [section 207(a) of Pub. L. 90–618][/us/pl/90/618/s207/a], set out as a note under [section 5801 of this title][/us/usc/t26/s5801].
