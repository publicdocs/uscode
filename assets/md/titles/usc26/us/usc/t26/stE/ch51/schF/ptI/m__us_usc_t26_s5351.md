## § 5351. Bonded wine cellar

    Any person establishing premises for the production, blending, cellar treatment, storage, bottling, packaging, or repackaging of untaxpaid wine (other than wine produced exempt from tax under section 5042), including the use of wine spirits in wine production, shall, before commencing operations, make application to the Secretary and file bond and receive permission to operate. Such premises shall be known as “bonded wine cellars”; except that any such premises engaging in production operations may, in the discretion of the Secretary, be designated as a “bonded winery”.

(Added [Pub. L. 85–859, title II, § 201][/us/pl/85/859/s201], Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1378][/us/stat/72/1378]; amended [Pub. L. 94–455, title XIX, § 1906(b)(13)(A)][/us/pl/94/455/s1906/b/13/A], Oct. 4, 1976, [90 Stat. 1834][/us/stat/90/1834].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    A prior section 5351, [act Aug. 16, 1954, ch. 736][/us/act/1954-08-16/ch736], [68A Stat. 663][/us/stat/68A/663], consisted of provisions similar to those comprising this section, prior to the general revision of this chapter by [Pub. L. 85–859][/us/pl/85/859].

 __Amendments__ 

    1976—[Pub. L. 94–455][/us/pl/94/455] struck out “or his delegate” after “Secretary” wherever appearing.

 __Effective Date__ 

    Section effective July 1, 1959, see [section 210(a)(1) of Pub. L. 85–859][/us/pl/85/859/s210/a/1], set out as a note under [section 5001 of this title][/us/usc/t26/s5001].
