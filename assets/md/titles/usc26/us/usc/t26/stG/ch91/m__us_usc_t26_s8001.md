## § 8001. Authorization

    There shall be a joint congressional committee known as the Joint Committee on Taxation (hereinafter in this subtitle referred to as the “Joint Committee”).

([Aug. 16, 1954, ch. 736][/us/act/1954-08-16/ch736], [68A Stat. 925][/us/stat/68A/925]; [Pub. L. 94–455, title XIX][/us/pl/94/455/tXIX], § 1907(a)(1), Oct. 4, 1976, [90 Stat. 1835][/us/stat/90/1835].)

 __Amendments__ 

    1976—[Pub. L. 94–455][/us/pl/94/455] struck out “Internal Revenue” after “Committee on”.

 __Effective Date of 1976 Amendment__ 

[Pub. L. 94–455, title XIX][/us/pl/94/455/tXIX], § 1907(c), Oct. 4, 1976, [90 Stat. 1836][/us/stat/90/1836], provided that: 

> “The amendments made by this section \[amending this section and sections 8004, 8021, and 8023 of this title and enacting provisions set out below\] shall take effect on the first day of the first month which begins more than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act \[Oct. 4, 1976\].”

 __References to Joint Committee on Internal Revenue Taxation__ 

[Pub. L. 94–455, title XIX][/us/pl/94/455/tXIX], § 1907(a)(5), Oct. 4, 1976, [90 Stat. 1836][/us/stat/90/1836], provided that: 

> “All references in any other statute, or in any rule, regulation, or order, to the Joint Committee on Internal Revenue Taxation shall be considered to be made to the Joint Committee on Taxation.”
