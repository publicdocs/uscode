## § 640d–8. Allotments in severalty to Paiute Indians now located on lands; issue of patents declaring United States as trustee

    Notwithstanding any other provision of this subchapter, the Secretary is authorized to allot in severalty to individual Paiute Indians, not now members of the Navajo Tribe, who are located within the area described in the Act of June 14, 1934 ([48 Stat. 960][/us/stat/48/960]), and who were located within such area, or are direct descendants of Paiute Indians who were located within such area, on the date of such Act, land in quantities as specified in section 331  <sup>\[1\]</sup>  <sup><sup> 1 See References in Text note below. </sup></sup>  of this title, and patents shall be issued to them for such lands having the legal effect and declaring that the United States holds such land in trust for the sole use and benefit of each allottee and, following his death, of his heirs according to the laws of the State of Arizona.

([Pub. L. 93–531, § 9][/us/pl/93/531/s9], Dec. 22, 1974, [88 Stat. 1716][/us/stat/88/1716].)

 __References in Text__ 

    Act of June 14, 1934, referred to in text, is [act June 14, 1934, ch. 521][/us/act/1934-06-14/ch521], [48 Stat. 960][/us/stat/48/960], which was not classified to the Code.

    [Section 331 of this title][/us/usc/t25/s331], referred to in text, was repealed by [Pub. L. 106–462, title I, § 106(a)(1)][/us/pl/106/462/s106/a/1], Nov. 7, 2000, [114 Stat. 2007][/us/stat/114/2007].
