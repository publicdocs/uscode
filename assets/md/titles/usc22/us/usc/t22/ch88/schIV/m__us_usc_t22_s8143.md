## § 8143. Specific enforcement

    (a) __Jurisdiction__ 

    The district courts of the United States shall have jurisdiction over civil actions brought by the head of an executive agency designated under [section 8111(a) of this title][/us/usc/t22/s8111/a]—

        (1) to restrain any conduct in violation of [section 8124 of this title][/us/usc/t22/s8124] or [section 8141 of this title][/us/usc/t22/s8141]; or

        (2) to compel the taking of any action required by or under this chapter or the Additional Protocol.

    (b) __Civil actions__ 

        (1) __In general__ 

        A civil action described in subsection (a) may be brought—

            (A) in the case of a civil action described in paragraph (1) of such subsection, in the United States district court for the judicial district in which any act, omission, or transaction constituting a violation of [section 8124 of this title][/us/usc/t22/s8124] or [section 8141 of this title][/us/usc/t22/s8141] occurred or in which the defendant is found or transacts business; or

            (B) in the case of a civil action described in paragraph (2) of such subsection, in the United States district court for the judicial district in which the defendant is found or transacts business.

        (2) __Service of process__ 

            In any such civil action, process shall be served on a defendant wherever the defendant may reside or may be found.

([Pub. L. 109–401, title II][/us/pl/109/401/tII], § 243, Dec. 18, 2006, [120 Stat. 2749][/us/stat/120/2749].)
