## § 1465b. Cuba Service

    The Broadcasting Board of Governors shall establish within the International Broadcasting Bureau a Cuba Service (hereafter in this section referred to as the “Service”). The Service shall be responsible for all radio broadcasts to Cuba authorized by [section 1465a of this title][/us/usc/t22/s1465a]. The Broadcasting Board of Governors shall appoint a head of the Service and shall employ such staff as the head of the Service may need to carry out his duties. The Cuba Service shall be administered separately from other Voice of America functions and the head of the Cuba Service shall report directly to the Board  <sup>\[1\]</sup>  <sup><sup> 1 So in original. There is no Board of the International Broadcasting Bureau. </sup></sup>  of the International Broadcasting Bureau.

([Pub. L. 98–111, § 4][/us/pl/98/111/s4], Oct. 4, 1983, [97 Stat. 750][/us/stat/97/750]; [Pub. L. 103–236, title III, § 305(e)(1)][/us/pl/103/236/s305/e/1], formerly § 305(d)(1), Apr. 30, 1994, [108 Stat. 436][/us/stat/108/436]; [Pub. L. 105–277, div. G][/us/pl/105/277], subdiv. A, title XIII, §§ 1323(f)(1), 1324(3), (4), (6), Oct. 21, 1998, [112 Stat. 2681–779][/us/stat/112/2681-779], 2681–781.)

 __Repeal of Section__ 

    Section repealed upon transmittal of determination by President under [section 6063(c)(3) of this title][/us/usc/t22/s6063/c/3] that democratically elected government in Cuba is in power, see [section 6037(c) of this title][/us/usc/t22/s6037/c].

 __Codification__ 

    Section was enacted as part of the Radio Broadcasting to Cuba Act which comprises this subchapter, and not as part of the United States Information and Educational Exchange Act of 1948 which comprises this chapter.

 __Amendments__ 

    1998—[Pub. L. 105–277, § 1324(6)][/us/pl/105/277/s1324/6], substituted “Board” for “Director” in last sentence.

    [Pub. L. 105–277, § 1324(4)][/us/pl/105/277/s1324/4], which directed the amendment of this section by substituting “the International Broadcasting Bureau” for “the Voice of America”, was executed by making the substitution for text in two places to reflect the probable intent of Congress.

    [Pub. L. 105–277, § 1324(3)][/us/pl/105/277/s1324/3], which directed the substitution of “the Broadcasting Board of Governors” for “the Director of the United States Information Agency” each place it appears, was executed by substituting “Broadcasting Board of Governors” for “Director of the United States Information Agency” in two places, to reflect the probable intent of Congress.

    1994—[Pub. L. 103–236][/us/pl/103/236] substituted “of the Voice of America” for “and the Associate Director for Broadcasting of the United States Information Agency”.

 __Effective Date of 1998 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 105–277][/us/pl/105/277] effective Oct. 1, 1999, see [section 1301 of Pub. L. 105–277][/us/pl/105/277/s1301], set out as an Effective Date note under [section 6531 of this title][/us/usc/t22/s6531].
