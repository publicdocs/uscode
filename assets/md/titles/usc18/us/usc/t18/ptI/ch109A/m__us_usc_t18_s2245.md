## § 2245. Offenses resulting in death

    (a)  __ <sup>\[__  __1__  __\]</sup> __  __ <sup><sup> __  __1__  __ So in original. No subsec. (b) has been enacted.__  __ </sup></sup> __   __In General__  __.—__ 

    A person who, in the course of an offense under this chapter, or section 1591, 2251, 2251A, 2260, 2421, 2422, 2423, or 2425, murders an individual, shall be punished by death or imprisoned for any term of years or for life.

(Added [Pub. L. 103–322, title VI, § 60010(a)(2)][/us/pl/103/322/s60010/a/2], Sept. 13, 1994, [108 Stat. 1972][/us/stat/108/1972]; amended [Pub. L. 109–248, title II, § 206(a)(3)][/us/pl/109/248/s206/a/3], July 27, 2006, [120 Stat. 613][/us/stat/120/613].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    A prior section 2245 was renumbered [section 2246 of this title][/us/usc/t18/s2246].

 __Amendments__ 

    2006—[Pub. L. 109–248][/us/pl/109/248] amended section catchline and text generally. Prior to amendment, text read as follows: “A person who, in the course of an offense under this chapter, engages in conduct that results in the death of a person, shall be punished by death or imprisoned for any term of years or for life.”
