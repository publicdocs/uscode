## § 541. Entry of goods falsely classified

    Whoever knowingly effects any entry of goods, wares, or merchandise, at less than the true weight or measure thereof, or upon a false classification as to quality or value, or by the payment of less than the amount of duty legally due, shall be fined under this title or imprisoned not more than two years, or both.

([June 25, 1948, ch. 645][/us/act/1948-06-25/ch645], [62 Stat. 715][/us/stat/62/715]; [Pub. L. 103–322, title XXXIII, § 330016(1)(K)][/us/pl/103/322/s330016/1/K], Sept. 13, 1994, [108 Stat. 2147][/us/stat/108/2147].)

 __Historical and Revision Notes__ 

    Based on title 18, U.S.C., 1940 ed., § 126 ([Mar. 4, 1909, ch. 321, § 69][/us/act/1909-03-04/ch321/s69], [35 Stat. 1101][/us/stat/35/1101]).

    Reference to persons aiding, contained in words “or aid in effecting,” was omitted as unnecessary as such persons are made principals by [section 2 of this title][/us/usc/t18/s2].

    Changes were made in phraseology.

 __Amendments__ 

    1994—[Pub. L. 103–322][/us/pl/103/322] substituted “fined under this title” for “fined not more than $5,000”.
