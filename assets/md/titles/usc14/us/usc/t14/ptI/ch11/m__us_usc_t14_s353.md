## § 353. Compulsory retirement at age of sixty-two

    Any enlisted member who has reached the age of sixty-two shall be retired from active service.

([Aug. 4, 1949, ch. 393][/us/act/1949-08-04/ch393], [63 Stat. 520][/us/stat/63/520]; [Pub. L. 98–557, § 15(a)(3)(A)][/us/pl/98/557/s15/a/3/A], Oct. 30, 1984, [98 Stat. 2865][/us/stat/98/2865]; [Pub. L. 99–348, title II, § 205(b)(8)][/us/pl/99/348/s205/b/8], July 1, 1986, [100 Stat. 700][/us/stat/100/700].)

 __Historical and Revision Notes__ 

    Based on title 14, U.S.C., 1946 ed., § 173 ([Apr. 12, 1902, ch. 501, § 4][/us/act/1902-04-12/ch501/s4], [32 Stat. 100][/us/stat/32/100]).

    [Section 173 of title 14][/us/usc/t14/s173], U.S.C., 1946 ed., has been divided. That part dealing with enlisted men is placed in this section. That part dealing with commissioned officers is placed in [section 230 of this title][/us/usc/t14/s230]. That part dealing with warrant officers is placed in [section 303 of this title][/us/usc/t14/s303].

    The compulsory retirement age is changed from 64 to 62 in order to make it the same for enlisted men as for officers. 81st Congress, House Report No. 557.

 __Amendments__ 

    1986—[Pub. L. 99–348][/us/pl/99/348] struck out “, with retired pay of the grade or rating with which retired” after “active service”.

    1984—[Pub. L. 98–557][/us/pl/98/557] substituted reference to enlisted member for reference to enlisted man.
