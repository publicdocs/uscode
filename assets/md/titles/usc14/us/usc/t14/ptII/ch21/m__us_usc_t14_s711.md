## § 711. Exclusiveness of service

    No member of the Reserve, other than a temporary member, may be a member of another military organization. A temporary member of the Reserve who is a member of another military component shall, if ordered to active duty therein, be disenrolled as a temporary member of the Reserve.

(Added [Pub. L. 96–322][/us/pl/96/322], § 1, Aug. 4, 1980, [94 Stat. 1006][/us/stat/94/1006]; amended [Pub. L. 97–136][/us/pl/97/136], § 6(c)(1), (2), Dec. 29, 1981, [95 Stat. 1706][/us/stat/95/1706].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    Provisions similar to those in this section were contained in [section 757 of this title][/us/usc/t14/s757] prior to the complete revision of this chapter by [Pub. L. 96–322][/us/pl/96/322].

 __Amendments__ 

    1981—[Pub. L. 97–136][/us/pl/97/136] struck out provision that a member of the Reserve, other than a temporary member, is exempt from registration and liability for military training and service under any other law, and substituted “Exclusiveness of service” for “Exemption from military training and draft; exclusiveness of service” in section catchline.
