## § 2606. Exempted transactions

    (a) __In general__ 

    This chapter does not apply to credit transactions involving extensions of credit—

        (1) primarily for business, commercial, or agricultural purposes; or

        (2) to government or governmental agencies or instrumentalities.

    (b) __Interpretation__ 

        In prescribing regulations under [section 2617(a) of this title][/us/usc/t12/s2617/a], the Bureau shall ensure that, with respect to subsection (a) of this section, the exemption for credit transactions involving extensions of credit primarily for business, commercial, or agricultural purposes, as provided in subsection (a)(1)  <sup>\[1\]</sup>  <sup><sup> 1 See References in Text note below. </sup></sup>  of this section shall be the same as the exemption for such credit transactions under [section 1603(1) of title 15][/us/usc/t15/s1603/1].

([Pub. L. 93–533, § 7][/us/pl/93/533/s7], as added [Pub. L. 103–325, title III, § 312][/us/pl/103/325/s312], Sept. 23, 1994, [108 Stat. 2221][/us/stat/108/2221]; amended [Pub. L. 104–208, div. A, title II, § 2103(b)][/us/pl/104/208/s2103/b], Sept. 30, 1996, [110 Stat. 3009–399][/us/stat/110/3009-399]; [Pub. L. 111–203, title X, § 1098(5)][/us/pl/111/203/s1098/5], July 21, 2010, [124 Stat. 2104][/us/stat/124/2104].)

 __References in Text__ 

    Subsection (a)(1) of this section, referred to in subsec. (b), was in the original “section 7(1) of the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act of 1974”, and was translated as referring to section 7(a)(1) of that Act to reflect the probable intent of Congress.
