## § 703. Allotments: members of Coast Guard

    Members of the Coast Guard may, under regulations prescribed by the Secretary of Homeland Security, make allotments from their pay and allowances.

([Pub. L. 87–649][/us/pl/87/649], Sept. 7, 1962, [76 Stat. 484][/us/stat/76/484]; [Pub. L. 90–623, § 3(1)][/us/pl/90/623/s3/1], Oct. 22, 1968, [82 Stat. 1314][/us/stat/82/1314]; [Pub. L. 107–296, title XVII, § 1704(c)][/us/pl/107/296/s1704/c], Nov. 25, 2002, [116 Stat. 2314][/us/stat/116/2314].)

    The words “members of the Coast Guard” are substituted for the words “officers and enlisted men”, since together they compose the entire membership.

 __Amendments__ 

    2002—[Pub. L. 107–296][/us/pl/107/296] substituted “Secretary of Homeland Security” for “Secretary of Transportation”.

    1968—[Pub. L. 90–623][/us/pl/90/623] substituted “Secretary of Transportation” for “Secretary of the Treasury”.

 __Effective Date of 2002 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 107–296][/us/pl/107/296] effective on the date of transfer of the Coast Guard to the Department of Homeland Security, see [section 1704(g) of Pub. L. 107–296][/us/pl/107/296/s1704/g], set out as a note under [section 101 of Title 10][/us/usc/t10/s101], Armed Forces.

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 90–623][/us/pl/90/623] intended to restate without substantive change the law in effect on Oct. 22, 1968, see [section 6 of Pub. L. 90–623][/us/pl/90/623/s6], set out as an Effective Date of 1968 Amendment note under [section 5334 of Title 5][/us/usc/t5/s5334], Government Organization and Employees.
