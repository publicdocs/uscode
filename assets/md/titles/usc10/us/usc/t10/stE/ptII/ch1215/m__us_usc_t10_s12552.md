## § 12552. Funeral honors functions at funerals for veterans

    Performance by a Reserve of funeral honors functions at the funeral of a veteran (as defined in [section 1491(h) of this title][/us/usc/t10/s1491/h]) may not be considered to be a period of drill or training, but may be performed as funeral honors duty under [section 12503 of this title][/us/usc/t10/s12503].

(Added [Pub. L. 105–261, div. A, title V][/us/pl/105/261/dA/tV], § 567(c)(1), Oct. 17, 1998, [112 Stat. 2031][/us/stat/112/2031]; amended [Pub. L. 106–65, div. A, title V][/us/pl/106/65/dA/tV], § 578(g)(4), Oct. 5, 1999, [113 Stat. 628][/us/stat/113/628]; [Pub. L. 106–398][/us/pl/106/398], § 1 \[\[div. A\], title X, § 1087(a)(21)\], Oct. 30, 2000, [114 Stat. 1654][/us/stat/114/1654], 1654A–291.)

 __Amendments__ 

    2000—[Pub. L. 106–398][/us/pl/106/398] inserted period at end.

    1999—[Pub. L. 106–65][/us/pl/106/65] substituted “honors functions at funerals for veterans” for “honor guard functions: prohibition of treatment as drill or training” in section catchline and amended text generally. Prior to amendment, text read as follows: “Performance by a Reserve of honor guard functions at the funeral of a veteran may not be considered to be a period of drill or training otherwise required.”
