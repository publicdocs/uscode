## § 1095f. TRICARE program: referrals for specialty health care

    The Secretary of Defense shall ensure that no contract for managed care support under the TRICARE program includes any requirement that a managed care support contractor require a primary care or specialty care provider to obtain prior authorization before referring a patient to a specialty care provider that is part of the network of health care providers or institutions of the contractor.

(Added [Pub. L. 106–398][/us/pl/106/398], § 1 \[\[div. A\], title VII, § 728(a)(1)\], Oct. 30, 2000, [114 Stat. 1654][/us/stat/114/1654], 1654A–189.)

 __Effective Date__ 

[Pub. L. 106–398][/us/pl/106/398], § 1 \[\[div. A\], title VII, § 728(c)\], Oct. 30, 2000, [114 Stat. 1654][/us/stat/114/1654], 1654A–189, provided that: 

> “[Section 1095f of title 10][/us/usc/t10/s1095f], United States Code, as added by subsection (a), shall apply with respect to a TRICARE managed care support contract entered into by the Department of Defense after the date of the enactment of this Act \[Oct. 30, 2000\].”
