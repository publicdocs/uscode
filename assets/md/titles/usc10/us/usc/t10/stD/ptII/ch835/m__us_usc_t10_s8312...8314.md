## \[§§ 8312 to 8314. Repealed. [Pub. L. 96–513, title II][/us/pl/96/513/tII], § 204, Dec. 12, 1980, [94 Stat. 2880][/us/stat/94/2880]\]

    Section 8312, [act Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041][/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041], [70A Stat. 519][/us/stat/70A/519], provided that an officer who is promoted in Regular Air Force is considered to have accepted his promotion on date of order announcing it, unless he expressly declines it, without need to take the oath of office upon promotion if his service since last taking it has been continuous. See [section 626 of this title][/us/usc/t10/s626].

    Section 8313, [act Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041][/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041], [70A Stat. 519][/us/stat/70A/519], provided that in time of war or national emergency declared by Congress or President, the President may suspend operation of any provision of law relating to promotion, mandatory retirement, or separation of commissioned officers of Regular Air Force. See section 123(a), (b) of this title.

    Section 8314, added [Pub. L. 85–861][/us/pl/85/861], § 1(177)(A), Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1519][/us/stat/72/1519], provided that promotion to a higher grade of a commissioned officer of Regular Air Force who is on a recommendation list awaiting promotion not be withheld or delayed because of original appointment of any other person to a commissioned grade in Regular Air Force and that this section does not apply to appointments as medical or dental officers or Air Force nurses or medical specialists. See [section 624 of this title][/us/usc/t10/s624].

 __Effective Date of Repeal__ 

    Repeal effective Sept. 15, 1981, see [section 701 of Pub. L. 96–513][/us/pl/96/513/s701], set out as an Effective Date of 1980 Amendment note under [section 101 of this title][/us/usc/t10/s101].
