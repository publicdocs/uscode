## § 2078. Certification of reason for advance

    A certificate by the Commissioner of U.S. Customs and Border Protection stating the amount of an expenditure made from funds advanced and certifying that the confidential nature of the transaction involved renders it inadvisable to specify the details thereof or impracticable to furnish the payee’s receipt shall be a sufficient voucher for the sum expressed to have been expended.

([Mar. 28, 1928, ch. 266][/us/act/1928-03-28/ch266], § 3, as added [Aug. 7, 1939, ch. 566][/us/act/1939-08-07/ch566], § 1, [53 Stat. 1263][/us/stat/53/1263]; amended [Pub. L. 91–513, title III][/us/pl/91/513/tIII], § 1102(n)(2), Oct. 27, 1970, [84 Stat. 1293][/us/stat/84/1293]; [Pub. L. 114–125, title VIII][/us/pl/114/125/tVIII], § 802(d)(2), Feb. 24, 2016, [130 Stat. 210][/us/stat/130/210].)

 __Codification__ 

    Section was formerly classified to [section 529d of Title 31][/us/usc/t31/s529d] prior to the general revision and enactment of Title 31, Money and Finance, by [Pub. L. 97–258][/us/pl/97/258], § 1, Sept. 13, 1982, [96 Stat. 877][/us/stat/96/877].

 __Amendments__ 

    1970—[Pub. L. 91–513][/us/pl/91/513] struck out reference to Commissioner of Narcotics.

 __Change of Name__ 

    “Commissioner of U.S. Customs and Border Protection” substituted for “Commissioner of Customs” in text on authority of [section 802(d)(2) of Pub. L. 114–125][/us/pl/114/125/s802/d/2], set out as a note under [section 211 of Title 6][/us/usc/t6/s211], Domestic Security.

 __Effective Date of 1970 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 91–513][/us/pl/91/513] effective on first day of seventh calendar month that begins after the day immediately preceding the date of enactment of [Pub. L. 91–513][/us/pl/91/513], which was approved on Oct. 27, 1970, see [section 1105(a) of Pub. L. 91–513][/us/pl/91/513/s1105/a], set out as an Effective Date note under [section 951 of Title 21][/us/usc/t21/s951], Food and Drugs.

 __Savings Provision__ 

    Prosecutions for any violation of law occurring, and civil seizures or forfeitures and injunctive proceedings commenced, prior to the effective date of amendment of this section by [section 1102 of Pub. L. 91–513][/us/pl/91/513/s1102] not to be affected or abated by reason thereof, see [section 1103 of Pub. L. 91–513][/us/pl/91/513/s1103], set out as a note under [section 171 of Title 21][/us/usc/t21/s171], Food and Drugs.

 __Transfer of Functions__ 

    For transfer of functions, personnel, assets, and liabilities of the United States Customs Service of the Department of the Treasury, including functions of the Secretary of the Treasury relating thereto, to the Secretary of Homeland Security, and for treatment of related references, see sections 203(1), 551(d), 552(d), and 557 of Title 6, Domestic Security, and the Department of Homeland Security Reorganization Plan of November 25, 2002, as modified, set out as a note under [section 542 of Title 6][/us/usc/t6/s542].

    For transfer of functions of other officers, employees, and agencies of Department of the Treasury, with certain exceptions, to Secretary of the Treasury, with power to delegate, see Reorg. Plan No. 26 of 1950, §§ 1, 2, eff. July 31, 1950, 15 F.R. 4935, [64 Stat. 1280][/us/stat/64/1280], 1281, set out in the Appendix to Title 5, Government Organization and Employees.
