## § 1652. Separability

    If any provision of this chapter, or the application thereof to any person or circumstances, is held invalid, the remainder of the chapter, and the application of such provision to other persons or circumstances, shall not be affected thereby.

([June 17, 1930, ch. 497][/us/act/1930-06-17/ch497], title IV, § 652, [46 Stat. 763][/us/stat/46/763].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    Provisions similar to those in this section were contained in [act Sept. 21, 1922, ch. 356, title IV][/us/act/1922-09-21/ch356/tIV], § 645, [42 Stat. 990][/us/stat/42/990]. That section was superseded by section 652 of act June 17, 1930, comprising this section, and repealed by section 651(a)(1) of the 1930 act.

 __Customs Procedural Reform and Simplification Act of 1978__ 

[Pub. L. 95–410, title IV][/us/pl/95/410/tIV], § 401, Oct. 3, 1978, [92 Stat. 905][/us/stat/92/905], provided that: 

> “If any provision of this Act \[see Short Title of 1978 Amendment note set out under [section 1654 of this title][/us/usc/t19/s1654]\], or the application thereof to any person or circumstances, is held invalid, the remainder of the provisions of this Act and the application of such provisions to other persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby.”
