## § 1881. Normal trade relations

    Except as otherwise provided in this subchapter, in [section 1351 of this title][/us/usc/t19/s1351], or in section 401(a) of the Tariff Classification Act of 1962, any duty or other import restriction or duty-free treatment proclaimed in carrying out any trade agreement under this subchapter or [section 1351 of this title][/us/usc/t19/s1351] shall apply to products of all foreign countries, whether imported directly or indirectly.

([Pub. L. 87–794, title II, § 251][/us/pl/87/794/s251], Oct. 11, 1962, [76 Stat. 879][/us/stat/76/879]; [Pub. L. 105–206, title V, § 5003(b)(1)][/us/pl/105/206/s5003/b/1], July 22, 1998, [112 Stat. 789][/us/stat/112/789].)

 __References in Text__ 

    Section 401(a) of the Tariff Classification Act of 1962, referred to in text, is set out as a note under [section 1351 of this title][/us/usc/t19/s1351].

 __Amendments__ 

    1998—[Pub. L. 105–206][/us/pl/105/206] substituted existing catchline for “Most-favored-nation principle”.
