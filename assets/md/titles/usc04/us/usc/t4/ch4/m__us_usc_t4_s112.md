## § 112. Compacts between States for cooperation in prevention of crime; consent of Congress

    (a) The consent of Congress is hereby given to any two or more States to enter into agreements or compacts for cooperative effort and mutual assistance in the prevention of crime and in the enforcement of their respective criminal laws and policies, and to establish such agencies, joint or otherwise, as they may deem desirable for making effective such agreements and compacts.

    (b) For the purpose of this section, the term “States” means the several States and Alaska, Hawaii, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, Guam, and the District of Columbia.

(Added [May 24, 1949, ch. 139][/us/act/1949-05-24/ch139], § 129(b), [63 Stat. 107][/us/stat/63/107], § 112, formerly § 111; amended [Aug. 3, 1956, ch. 941][/us/act/1956-08-03/ch941], [70 Stat. 1020][/us/stat/70/1020]; [Pub.  __Historical and Revision Notes__ 

    This section \[section 129(b) of Act May 24, 1949\] incorporates in title 4, U.S.C. (enacted into positive law by act of July 30, 1947 (ch. 389, § 1, [61 Stat. 641][/us/stat/61/641]), the provisions of former [section 420 of title 18][/us/usc/t18/s420], U.S.C. ([act of June 6, 1934, ch. 406][/us/act/1934-06-06/ch406], [48 Stat. 909][/us/stat/48/909]), which, in the course of the revision of such title 18, was omitted therefrom and recommended for transfer to such title 4. (See table 7—Transferred sections, p. A219, H. Rept. No. 304, April 24, 1947, to accompany H.R. 3190, 80th Cong.).

 __Amendments__ 

    1962—Subsec. (b). [Pub. L. 87–406][/us/pl/87/406] inserted “Guam” after “the Virgin Islands,”.

    1956—Act Aug. 3, 1956, designated existing provisions as subsec. (a) and added subsec. (b).

For Alaska Statehood Law, see [Pub. L. 85–508][/us/pl/85/508], July 7, 1958, [72 Stat. 339][/us/stat/72/339], set out as a note preceding former [section 21 of Title 48][/us/usc/t48/s21], Territories and Insular Possessions.
