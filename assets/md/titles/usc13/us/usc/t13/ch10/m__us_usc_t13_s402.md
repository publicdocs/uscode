## § 402. Providing business data to Designated Statistical Agencies

    The Bureau of the Census may provide business data to the Bureau of Economic Analysis and the Bureau of Labor Statistics (“Designated Statistical Agencies”) if such information is required for an authorized statistical purpose and the provision is the subject of a written agreement with that Designated Statistical Agency, or their successors, as defined in the Confidential Information Protection and Statistical Efficiency Act of 2002.

(Added [Pub. L. 107–347, title V][/us/pl/107/347/tV], § 526(b)(1), Dec. 17, 2002, [116 Stat. 2969][/us/stat/116/2969].)

 __References in Text__ 

    The Confidential Information Protection and Statistical Efficiency Act of 2002, referred to in text, is title V of [Pub. L. 107–347][/us/pl/107/347], Dec. 17, 2002, [116 Stat. 2962][/us/stat/116/2962], which enacted this section, amended [section 176a of Title 15][/us/usc/t15/s176a], Commerce and Trade, and enacted provisions set out as a note under [section 3501 of Title 44][/us/usc/t44/s3501], Public Printing and Documents. Provisions defining “Designated Statistical Agency” are contained in section 522 of the Act, which is set out in a note under [section 3501 of Title 44][/us/usc/t44/s3501].
