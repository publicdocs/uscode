## § 99. Maine

    Maine constitutes one judicial district.

    Court shall be held at Bangor and Portland.

([June 25, 1948, ch. 646][/us/act/1948-06-25/ch646], [62 Stat. 881][/us/stat/62/881]; [Pub. L. 95–573][/us/pl/95/573], § 2, Nov. 2, 1978, [92 Stat. 2458][/us/stat/92/2458].)

 __Historical and Revision Notes__ 

    Based on title 28, U.S.C., 1940 ed., § 160 ([Mar. 3, 1911, ch. 231][/us/act/1911-03-03/ch231], § 85, [36 Stat. 1114][/us/stat/36/1114]; [Dec. 22, 1911, ch. 7][/us/act/1911-12-22/ch7], [37 Stat. 51][/us/stat/37/51]; [Sept. 8, 1916, ch. 475][/us/act/1916-09-08/ch475], §§ 1, 3, [39 Stat. 850][/us/stat/39/850]; [Mar. 4, 1923, ch. 279][/us/act/1923-03-04/ch279], [42 Stat. 1506][/us/stat/42/1506]).

    Changes in arrangement and phraseology were made.

 __Amendments__ 

    1978—[Pub. L. 95–573][/us/pl/95/573] struck out provision for two separate divisions, (1) the Northern Division comprising the counties of Aroostook, Hancock, Penobscot, Piscataquis, Somerset, Waldo, and Washington and (2) the Southern Division comprising the counties of Androscoggin, Cumberland, Franklin, Kennebec, Knox, Lincoln, Oxford, Sagadahoc, and York.

 __Effective Date of 1978 Amendment__ 

    Amendment by [Pub. L. 95–573][/us/pl/95/573] effective 180 days after Nov. 2, 1978, see [section 6 of Pub. L. 95–573][/us/pl/95/573/s6], set out as a note under [section 93 of this title][/us/usc/t28/s93].
