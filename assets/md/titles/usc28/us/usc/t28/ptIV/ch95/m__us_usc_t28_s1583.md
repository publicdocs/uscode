## § 1583. Counterclaims, cross-claims, and third-party actions

    In any civil action in the Court of International Trade, the court shall have exclusive jurisdiction to render judgment upon any counterclaim, cross-claim, or third-party action of any party, if (1) such claim or action involves the imported merchandise that is the subject matter of such civil action, or (2) such claim or action is to recover upon a bond or customs duties relating to such merchandise.

(Added [Pub. L. 96–417, title II][/us/pl/96/417/tII], § 201, Oct. 10, 1980, [94 Stat. 1729][/us/stat/94/1729].)

 __Prior Provisions__ 

    A prior section 1583, [act June 25, 1948, ch. 646][/us/act/1948-06-25/ch646], [62 Stat. 943][/us/stat/62/943], related to certain cases of exclusive jurisdiction of the Customs Court, prior to repeal by [Pub. L. 91–271, title I][/us/pl/91/271/tI], § 111, June 2, 1970, [84 Stat. 278][/us/stat/84/278].

 __Effective Date__ 

    Section applicable with respect to civil actions commenced on or after Nov. 1, 1980, see [section 701(b)(1)(A) of Pub. L. 96–417][/us/pl/96/417/s701/b/1/A], set out as an Effective Date of 1980 Amendment note under [section 251 of this title][/us/usc/t28/s251].
