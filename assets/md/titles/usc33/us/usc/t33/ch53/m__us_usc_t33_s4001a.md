## § 4001a. Consultation required

    In developing the assessments, reports, and plans under the amendments made by this title, <sup>\[1\]</sup>  <sup><sup> 1 See References in Text note below. </sup></sup>  the Task Force shall consult with the coastal States, Indian tribes, local governments, appropriate industries (including fisheries, agriculture, and fertilizer), academic institutions, and nongovernmental organizations with expertise in coastal zone science and management.

([Pub. L. 108–456, title I][/us/pl/108/456/tI], § 102 (part), Dec. 10, 2004, [118 Stat. 3630][/us/stat/118/3630].)

 __References in Text__ 

    This title, referred to in text, means title I of [Pub. L. 108–456][/us/pl/108/456], Dec. 10, 2004, [118 Stat. 3630][/us/stat/118/3630], known as the Harmful Algal Bloom and Hypoxia Amendments Act of 2004, which enacted this section, amended sections 4001 and 4005 of this title, and enacted provisions set out as a note under [section 4001 of this title][/us/usc/t33/s4001]. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see Short Title of 2004 Amendment note set out under [section 4001 of this title][/us/usc/t33/s4001] and Tables.

 __Codification__ 

    Section is comprised of part of [section 102 of Pub. L. 108–456][/us/pl/108/456/s102]. Remainder of section 102 amended [section 4001 of this title][/us/usc/t33/s4001].

    Section was enacted as part of the Harmful Algal Bloom and Hypoxia Amendments Act of 2004, and not as part of the Harmful Algal Bloom and Hypoxia Research and Control Act of 1998 which comprises this chapter.

    Section was formerly set out as a note under [section 1451 of Title 16][/us/usc/t16/s1451], Conservation.
