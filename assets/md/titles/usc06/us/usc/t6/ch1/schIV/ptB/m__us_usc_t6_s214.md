## § 214. Separate budget request for Customs

    The President shall include in each budget transmitted to Congress under [section 1105 of title 31][/us/usc/t31/s1105] a separate budget request for the United States Customs Service.

([Pub. L. 107–296, title IV, § 414][/us/pl/107/296/s414], Nov. 25, 2002, [116 Stat. 2180][/us/stat/116/2180].)

 __Change of Name__ 

    Customs Service, referred to in text, changed to Bureau of Customs and Border Protection by Reorganization Plan Modification for the Department of Homeland Security, eff. Mar. 1, 2003, H. Doc. No. 108–32, 108th Congress, 1st Session, set out as a note under [section 542 of this title][/us/usc/t6/s542].

 __Land Border Projects__ 

[Pub. L. 112–74, div. D, title II][/us/pl/112/74], Dec. 23, 2011, [125 Stat. 949][/us/stat/125/949], provided in part: 

> “That for fiscal year 2012 and thereafter, the annual budget submission of U.S. Customs and Border Protection for ‘Construction and Facilities Management’ shall, in consultation with the General Services Administration, include a detailed 5-year plan for all Federal land border port of entry projects with a yearly update of total projected future funding needs delineated by land port of entry”.

Similar provisions were contained in the following prior appropriation acts:

    [Pub. L. 111–83, title II][/us/pl/111/83], Oct. 28, 2009, [123 Stat. 2148][/us/stat/123/2148].

    [Pub. L. 110–329, div. D, title II][/us/pl/110/329], Sept. 30, 2008, [122 Stat. 3658][/us/stat/122/3658].
