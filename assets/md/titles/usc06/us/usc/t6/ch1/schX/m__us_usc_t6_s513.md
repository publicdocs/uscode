## § 513. Federal air marshal program

    (1) __Sense of Congress__ 

        It is the sense of Congress that the Federal air marshal program is critical to aviation security.

    (2) __Limitation on statutory construction__ 

        Nothing in this chapter, including any amendment made by this chapter, shall be construed as preventing the Under Secretary of Transportation for Security from implementing and training Federal air marshals.

([Pub. L. 107–296, title XIV][/us/pl/107/296/tXIV], § 1402(c), Nov. 25, 2002, [116 Stat. 2305][/us/stat/116/2305].)

 __References in Text__ 

    This chapter, referred to in par. (2), was in the original “this Act”, meaning [Pub. L. 107–296][/us/pl/107/296], Nov. 25, 2002, [116 Stat. 2135][/us/stat/116/2135], known as the Homeland Security Act of 2002, which is classified principally to this chapter.
