## § 742. National preparedness

    In order to prepare the Nation for all hazards, including natural disasters, acts of terrorism, and other man-made disasters, the President, consistent with the declaration of policy under [section 5195 of title 42][/us/usc/t42/s5195] and title V of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 ([6 U.S.C. 311][/us/usc/t6/s311] et seq.), as amended by this Act, shall develop a national preparedness goal and a national preparedness system.

([Pub. L. 109–295, title VI][/us/pl/109/295/tVI], § 642, Oct. 4, 2006, [120 Stat. 1425][/us/stat/120/1425].)

 __References in Text__ 

    The Homeland Security Act of 2002, referred to in text, is [Pub. L. 107–296][/us/pl/107/296], Nov. 25, 2002, [116 Stat. 2135][/us/stat/116/2135], as amended. Title V of the Act is classified generally to subchapter V (§ 311 et seq.) of chapter 1 of this title. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see Short Title note set out under [section 101 of this title][/us/usc/t6/s101] and Tables.

    This Act, referred to in text, means title VI of [Pub. L. 109–295][/us/pl/109/295], Oct. 4, 2006, [120 Stat. 1394][/us/stat/120/1394], known as the Post-Katrina Emergency Management Reform Act of 2006. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see Short Title and References in [Pub. L. 109–295][/us/pl/109/295] notes set out under [section 701 of this title][/us/usc/t6/s701] and Tables.
