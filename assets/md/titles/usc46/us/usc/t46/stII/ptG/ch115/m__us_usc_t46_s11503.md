## § 11503. Duties of consular officers related to insubordination

    (a) A consular officer shall use every means to discountenance insubordination on vessels of the United States, including employing the aid of local authorities.

    (b) When a seaman is accused of insubordination, a consular officer shall inquire into the facts and proceed as provided in [section 11106 of this title][/us/usc/t46/s11106]. If the consular officer discharges the seaman, the officer shall endorse the agreement required by this part and enter in the vessel’s official logbook the cause and particulars of the discharge.

([Pub. L. 98–89][/us/pl/98/89], Aug. 26, 1983, [97 Stat. 583][/us/stat/97/583].)
